# Achievement Philosophy

> Design canon. Achievements in *Sakura Kills Everything* are not score milestones. They are
> **memories with names.** A good achievement does not say "you did a number." It says "you
> were there for a moment that became a story." The achievement system is, secretly, a second
> incident archive — one the player writes by playing.

---

## The Core Principle

**Achievements should create memories, not measure statistics.**

The wrong instinct is to reward *quantity*: catch 50 birds, complete 100 patrols, walk
10,000 steps. These are real and they are dead. No one remembers their fifty-first bird.

The right instinct is to reward *story*: the one that got away, the day history changed, the
suspicious leaf that turned out to be a leaf. These are small, specific, and unforgettable —
because they sound like something Tanisha would write down.

| ❌ Statistic (do not) | ✅ Story (do) |
|---|---|
| Catch 50 Birds | **The One That Got Away** — a bird escaped by a whisker |
| Complete 100 Patrols | **The Patio Defender** — held the throne room against a raid |
| Chase 500 Squirrels | **Zero and Counting** — reach a perfect, glorious 0-for-100 |
| Walk 10,000 steps | **The Day History Changed** — witness a confirmed catch |
| Investigate 25 objects | **Suspicious Leaf Investigator** — file a full report on a leaf |

Every achievement name should pass the test: *could this be the title of an incident in the
archive?* If yes, it belongs. If it sounds like a stat counter, rewrite it.

---

## The Five Categories

Achievements are organized not by difficulty but by **the kind of memory they make.**

### 1. 🏅 Legends — *The Big Moments*
Rare, momentous, story-defining. The achievements you tell people about.
- **The Day History Changed** — Witness a confirmed catch. (The Bird Incident, relived.)
- **Warden of the Year** — Complete a full seasonal cycle of patrols.
- **The Long Watch** — Hold the Dusk Vigil through winter's longest night.
- **Birds Must Die (One Did)** — The bird-mode capstone. Spoken of in hushed tones.

### 2. 😅 Glorious Failures — *Victory in Defeat*
The heart of the game. Per canon law, the narrator finds the win. These celebrate the loss.
- **The One That Got Away** — A target escapes by the narrowest possible margin.
- **Zero and Counting** — Reach 0 squirrel catches across 100 sincere attempts. A career.
- **The Field Was Secured (It Was Not)** — Declare a hundred victories that changed nothing.
- **Out-Stared by a Rabbit** — Lose a staring contest with General Thistle.
- **Defeated by a Smell** — Abandon a sure pursuit to investigate the garden.

### 3. 🔍 Investigations — *Sincere Attention to Nothing*
Reward curiosity and total commitment to trivial mysteries.
- **Suspicious Leaf Investigator** — Conduct a full investigation of a leaf.
- **The Hose Remains Dormant** — Keep watch over a threat that does not exist.
- **Tracks in the Snow** — Document a full set of unidentified winter prints.
- **The Blurry Photo** — Capture vorg "evidence" that resolves nothing. (See below.)

### 4. 🤝 Diplomacy & Reputation — *The Politics of the Realm*
Reward engagement with the factions and the slow shifts in standing.
- **The Truce of Old Greycoat** — Let the old squirrel be.
- **Cold Peace** — Hold the border with the Neighbor's Cat without either side crossing.
- **Recognized by the Patio Authority** — Earn an official commendation (paperwork).
- **Friend of the Firefly Court** — Be present for the summer Court without interfering.

### 5. 💛 Wholesome — *Joy for Its Own Sake*
No conflict. No enemy. Pure happiness. As important as any Legend.
- **The Forgotten Toy** — Recover a toy lost to history.
- **First Snow** — Experience the year's first snow patrol.
- **The Sun Patch** — Track the warmth across the field for a full afternoon.
- **Too Small to Hunt** — Encounter the Kits and do absolutely nothing about it.

## Design Rules for Achievements

1. **Name it like an incident.** The title carries the story. Lead with the title; the
   description is the footnote.
2. **Reward the moment, not the grind.** If it can be earned by repetition alone, it is a
   stat in disguise. Rewrite it around the *first* or *most extreme* instance.
3. **Celebrate failure honestly.** The Glorious Failures category is the game's signature.
   Never make the player feel bad for losing — make losing *legendary.*
4. **Earn it through play, log it in canon.** A meaningful achievement should be eligible to
   become a real `incident-archive.md` entry. The two systems feed each other.
5. **No achievement mocks Sakura or the player.** "Uh oh, missed again!" is forbidden (see
   `narrative-voice-guide.md`). The frame is always earnest and warm.
6. **Wholesome is not filler.** At least one in five achievements should have no enemy in it
   at all. The joy is the point of the whole project.

---

## The Vorg Exception

Vorg-related achievements may **never** confirm the vorg. They reward *curiosity*, not
*conclusion*:
- **The Blurry Photo**, **The Cold Spot**, **Sketch C** (earn the blank-page "best evidence").
- A long-running meta-achievement, **The Believer**, that fills with evidence over months and
  *never completes* — a progress bar with no 100%, on purpose. The mystery is the reward.

---

## Family Achievements (Multiplayer / Participation)

Because the real audience is the family, some achievements belong to *people,* not just play:
- **The Witness** — Be the family member present when a real catch happens. (Tanisha holds
  the original.)
- **Mission Control** — Open the door / start the operational day.
- **Chaos Liaison** — Trigger a multi-creature event. (Nolyn's birthright.)
- **The Skeptic's Burden** — Doubt the vorg, on the record, and be neither proven right nor
  wrong.

These tie the game back to the people it is for. See `family-cast.md`.

---

## Achievement Law

- Memory over metric. Always.
- The title is the achievement; the number is just how you got there.
- Failure is the richest source of legend in the whole game. Mine it with affection.
- Keep the categories balanced — a game that is all Legends is exhausting; a game with no
  Wholesome is cold.
- An achievement a 7-year-old is proud of and an adult finds funny is a perfect achievement.
