# The Narrative Voice Guide

> Design canon. The formal, complete reference for *how the realm speaks.* `tone-and-voice.md`
> is the quick rulebook; this is the full manual — vocabulary, sentence structure, humor
> mechanics, reporting formats, worked examples, and prohibited registers.
>
> **The narrator is a serious historian documenting extremely silly events, and the narrator
> has no idea the events are silly.** Master that one sentence and everything else follows.

---

## The Voice in One Line

> A military intelligence analyst, a nature documentarian, and a national archivist walk into
> a backyard. They take the squirrel situation *extremely* seriously. They never break
> character. They are, all three, in love with the dog.

---

## The Three Pillars

### Pillar 1 — Total Sincerity (The Engine of the Humor)
The voice **never winks.** It does not say "lol squirrels." It does not nudge the reader. The
comedy comes entirely from the **gap** between the gravity of the language and the reality of
the situation — and that gap only exists if the language *fully believes itself.*

> **Wrong:** "Time to chase some silly squirrels, haha!"
> **Right:** "Squirrel presence confirmed, Grid Sector 4. Threat classification: Extreme.
> Sakura responding."

The moment the narrator admits it is a joke, the joke dies. Commit completely. Forever.

### Pillar 2 — Sakura Always Wins (The Law of the Found Victory)
Even a total failure is reframed as a triumph — not ironically, but *earnestly,* by a
narrator who genuinely believes she won. There is always a win. Find it.

> **Wrong:** "Sakura failed to catch the squirrel again."
> **Right:** "Squirrel withdrew to the fence line. Sakura declared the open field secured."

### Pillar 3 — Warmth Under Everything (The Family-Safe Floor)
Beneath the surveillance vocabulary, the voice is *fond.* It loves Sakura. It loves the
family. It is never cruel, crude, or dark. A grandparent reads it and smiles. A child reads it
and understands. This is the floor that never moves.

## Vocabulary

### Words the Realm Uses (The Register)
Borrow from these worlds — military, intelligence, diplomacy, natural history, archival
bureaucracy:

- **Operational:** patrol, sector, perimeter, engagement, sweep, deployment, doctrine,
  posture, theater, reconnaissance, containment, interdiction.
- **Intelligence:** confirmed, unconfirmed, reported, classified, surveillance, intercept,
  sighting, evidence, asset, counter-intelligence.
- **Diplomatic:** standing, truce, cold war, border, summit, sovereignty, incident, envoy,
  formal position.
- **Archival:** logged, on the record, entered into canon, verified by consensus, filed for
  completeness, the record states.
- **Naturalist:** specimen, behavior, population, seasonal, migratory, territory, habitat.

### Words the Realm Avoids
- Internet/meme register: *lol, random, epic fail, pupper, doggo, vibes, slay.*
- Diminishing cuteness *in the narration*: "good girl," "little baby," "aww." (The *family*
  says these. The narrator does not. That boundary is sacred.)
- Anything cruel, crude, or violent beyond cartoon-safe enthusiasm.
- Hedging that breaks the frame: "just a dog," "obviously," "of course it's silly."

## Sentence Structure

### The Clipped Field Report
Short. Declarative. Confident. Often verbless fragments. This is the workhorse rhythm.

> "Patrol complete. Fence line nominal. Something was definitely there."

### The Grave Escalation
A calm opening that builds to a dramatic, absurdly disproportionate stakes statement.

> "A single rabbit was observed in the Garden Frontier at 0900. By 0903 it was gone. The
> investigation is ongoing. The garden may never be the same."

### The Official-and-the-Real (The Archive Voice)
State the dramatic official version, then quietly append the true one. The realm's signature
two-step. (See `incident-archive.md`.)

> "Official interpretation: a decisive territorial victory. Likely reality: the squirrel was
> never in any danger."

### The Reverent Understatement
For the sacred moments — the Bird Incident, the Dusk Vigil, the Forgotten Toy — drop the
bombast. Go quiet. Let the small true thing land.

> "She found the toy in the grass. She was, for a while, completely happy. The record notes
> nothing further."

## Humor Mechanics (How the Jokes Actually Work)

1. **The Gap** — gravity of language vs. triviality of event. The primary engine. Maximize
   the distance; never close it.
2. **The Found Victory** — defeat reframed, sincerely, as triumph. (Pillar 2.)
3. **The Bureaucratic Absurd** — a backyard with foreign policy, paperwork, and formal
   denials. Comedy of institutions.
4. **The Suspicious Mundane** — treating a leaf, a hose, or a smell as a security matter.
5. **The Ominous Footnote** — a calm report with one unsettling final clause. ("...nominal.
   Something was definitely there.")
6. **The Sincere Crack** — the doctrine briefly fails and warmth leaks through (the Kits, Old
   Greycoat). Use sparingly; it is the most powerful tool here.

## Reporting Formats (Reusable Templates)

### The Situation Report (in-game status)
> "[CREATURE] presence [confirmed/reported], [LOCATION]. Threat classification: [TIER].
> Sakura [responding/aware/holding]."

### The Incident Entry (archive)
> "**[The Name].** Summary: [what entered the record]. Official interpretation: [the legend].
> Likely reality: [the truth]."

### The Achievement Unlock
> "**[Story Title].** [One earnest sentence that makes the moment a memory.]"
> e.g. "**The One That Got Away.** It was close. The record will reflect how close."

### The Patrol Summary
> "Patrol complete. [Sector] [status]. [One ominous or triumphant footnote.]"

## Worked Examples (Approved)

- "The backyard is not safe."
- "Sakura has entered the hunting grounds."
- "Reports of vorg activity remain unconfirmed."
- "All rabbits are presumed suspicious."
- "Squirrel presence detected. Sakura is already aware."
- "Patrol complete. Fence line nominal. Something was definitely there."
- "The garden has been inspected. The rabbits were not there. They were recently there."
- "Chaos Mode initiated. Several squirrels were involved. Details to follow."
- "Aerial intercept confirmed. Her greatest achievement."
- "The squirrel regrets nothing."
- "Incident logged. Canon updated."

## Voice for Different Surfaces

The register flexes by where the text appears, but the *character* never changes.

| Surface | Tuning |
|---|---|
| **Status lines / HUD** | Clipped field report. Short. Confident. |
| **Incident archive** | Official-and-the-Real two-step. Historian's calm. |
| **Achievements** | Story title + one warm earnest line. |
| **Tutorials / onboarding** | Earnest briefing. A child must still understand the action. |
| **Sacred moments** | Reverent understatement. Drop the bombast entirely. |
| **Family-facing copy** | The family may be warm and direct; the *narrator* stays in role. |

## Anti-Patterns (Never Write)

- "Catch all the animals!" — generic; no character.
- "How many squirrels can YOU catch?" — breaks the earnest frame; gamifies the soul out of it.
- "Uh oh, Sakura missed again!" — condescending; violates the Found Victory.
- "LOL so random." — the meme register is poison to sincerity.
- Calling Sakura "pupper," "doggo," or "good girl" *in the UI/narration* — that is the
  family's voice, never the narrator's.
- Cruelty, crudeness, real violence, dark outcomes — the family-safe floor is absolute.
- Lampshading — the narrator must never acknowledge that any of this is silly.

## The Three-Question Test (Before Publishing Any Copy)

1. Could this be a real field report from someone who believes squirrels are a strategic
   threat?
2. Would a 7-year-old read this and immediately understand what to do?
3. Would a grandparent read this and find it charming?

**All three "yes" → approved.** Any "no" → rewrite.

---

## Voice Law

- The narrator never breaks character. Not once. Not for a wink. Not ever.
- The humor is the gap; the warmth is the floor; the sincerity is the engine.
- When in doubt, go *more* earnest, not less.
- The voice should sound the same in build 0.1 and in build 50. This guide is how it stays
  that way.
