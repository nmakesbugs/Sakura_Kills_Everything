# Tone and Voice

---

## The Voice

The game speaks like a military briefing written by someone who takes squirrels extremely seriously.

It is official. It is earnest. It is completely aware that it is about a dog in a backyard, and it does not care.

---

## Core Voice Rules

### 1. Earnest, Not Ironic
The game does not wink. It does not say "lol squirrels." It says: "Squirrel presence confirmed in Grid Sector 4. Threat classification: Extreme. Sakura responding."

The humor comes from the gap between the gravity of the language and the reality of the situation. The language must believe itself completely.

### 2. Sakura Always Wins Eventually
Even if she missed the squirrel, she absolutely dominated that particular patch of grass. The copy should find the win. There is always a win.

**Bad:** "Sakura failed to catch the squirrel."  
**Good:** "Squirrel retreated to fence line. Sakura declared the open field secured."

### 3. All Rabbits Are Suspicious
Rabbits do not get the benefit of the doubt. The copy treats every rabbit as a person of interest in an ongoing investigation.

### 4. Vorgs Are Treated Seriously
Reports of vorg activity are logged with the same gravity as confirmed squirrel sightings. The uncertainty is noted, but it is not mocked. Something is back there.

### 5. Family-Safe Always
No crude language. No dark outcomes. No violence beyond cartoon-safe enthusiasm. A grandparent reads this and smiles.

---

## Reference Lines (Approved Tone)

These are examples of correct game voice:

- "The backyard is not safe."
- "Sakura has entered the hunting grounds."
- "Reports of vorg activity remain unconfirmed."
- "All rabbits are presumed suspicious."
- "Squirrel presence detected. Sakura is already aware."
- "Patrol complete. Fence line nominal. Something was definitely there."
- "The garden has been inspected. The rabbits were not there. They were recently there."
- "Chaos Mode initiated. Several squirrels were involved. Details to follow."
- "Incident logged. Canon updated."

---

## Anti-Patterns (Do Not Write)

- "Catch all the animals!" — too generic
- "How many squirrels can YOU catch?" — breaks the earnest frame
- "Uh oh, Sakura missed again!" — condescending
- "LOL so random" — no
- Anything that calls Sakura a "pupper" or "good girl" in the UI — that's family's job, not the game's

---

## Writing Test

Before publishing any in-game copy, ask:

1. Could this be a real field report from someone who believes squirrels are a strategic threat?
2. Would a 7-year-old read this and immediately understand what to do?
3. Would a grandparent read this and find it charming?

If all three: approved.
