# v0.7R — The Tanisha Cut (QA Note)

A note, not a thesis.

## 1. Why v0.7R exists
The platform was strong but the *game* was boring and system-heavy; the playable Sakura moment
stayed thin. v0.7R ignores the platform and builds **one rich, funny, playable episode** for
Tanisha. Make Tanisha smile.

## 2. What was intentionally cut
Everything systemic: Duck Hunt, Patrol, Permanent Record, storage, Canon Archive, incident
engine, adventure engine, filters, zone map, data files, the multi-mode app shell. None of it
is used by this build. It's a standalone comic at `tanisha-cut/index.html`.

## 3. What was reused
Only the **real Sakura photo** (static, framed — opening portrait only), the **official
interpretation vs. likely reality** device, **The Bird Incident** premise, the visual policy
(no animated photo; symbolic paws/feathers/stamps), and the Playwright setup. No code imports
from the existing app.

## 4. What this prototype is testing
Whether *Sakura Kills Everything* can be fun as a small authored family story-game — an
interactive comic with choices, a climax, and a screenshot-worthy report — instead of a systems
demo.

## 5. Why it should feel more joyful than the prior app
- It's a **story**, not a loop: 13 beats with pacing, reveals, and gags.
- **Three meaningful choices** (Stalk / Launch / Bark) each change the gag, the complication,
  Tanisha's note, the official line, the likely reality, and the outcome stamp.
- Visuals are **handcrafted panels** (backyard scene, tagged bird, paws, feather bursts, motion
  streak, bark bubble, a slammed stamp), not floating emoji in empty space.
- The **report is the payoff** — an official file you'll want to send someone.

## 6. What Tanisha should react to
- She is the **witness**, by name, in every run ("Tanisha looked outside at exactly the wrong
  time"), and her **witness note** is a featured, gold-boxed part of the final report.
- The likely-reality punchlines (e.g. *"Tanisha saw a blur, heard a thump, and then the yard had
  feathers in it"*) and the outcome stamps (Disciplined Victory / Immediate Force Authorized /
  Victory By Announcement).
- Replaying with a different approach to see how Sakura re-explains herself.

## 7. What to do if it still feels boring
Then the problem isn't the framework — it's that the *writing and pacing* need to carry it.
Double down on: more authored episodes (Squirrel Surveillance, Garden Frontier), sharper jokes,
a little real Sakura sprite art for the action beat, and Tanisha-specific lines. If the comic
format itself doesn't land, the project may simply be a *canon/lore* property to read, not play —
and that's worth knowing too.

## 8. Status / how to play
Open `tanisha-cut/index.html` directly (no server, no build). ~3–5 min first play, ~1–2 min
replays. Mobile portrait (390×700). Playwright: `tests/playwright/tanisha-cut.spec.js`. Labeled
**v0.7R** in the HUD; repo `package.json` version intentionally left unchanged — this is a
recovery prototype, not a platform release.
