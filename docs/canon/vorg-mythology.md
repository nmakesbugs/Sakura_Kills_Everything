# The Vorg Dossier

> What is a vorg? This is the correct question. It is also, after all this documentation,
> the only question we are sure of.
>
> Tier 1 canon. The Vorg is the foundational mystery of the entire mythology — the dark
> matter that gives the bright, silly backyard its depth. **This dossier exists to make the
> mystery larger, never smaller.** Nothing here resolves it. Nothing here ever should. The
> day the vorg is explained is the day the backyard stops being infinite.

## The Core Evidence: The Dusk Vigil

Everything begins here. Strip away the theories and the rumors and one stubborn fact remains:

**Sakura — who challenges thunder, confronts lawnmowers, and fears no living creature in the
realm — will not commit to the far corner at dusk.**

Every evening she faces the Unknown Regions, holds the line, and returns. She does not flee.
She does not advance. She *holds.* This single, consistent, deeply uncharacteristic restraint
from the most fearless resident of the backyard is the foundation of the entire vorg
question. Whatever is out there, she perceives it, and she has decided — alone, wordlessly —
that tonight is not the night.

She has decided this every night for years.

---

## Known Facts (Confirmed)

These are the only things the realm will state plainly.

- The word **"vorg"** exists in the family vocabulary. It refers to *something.* Possibly
  several somethings.
- Sakura demonstrates anomalous, repeatable behavior near the Unknown Regions consistent with
  detection of *a presence.*
- Vorgs, if real, are **not** squirrels, **not** birds, **not** rabbits, **not** the cat.
  They are a separate category. They are *else.*
- No vorg has ever been directly observed by a human. Every datum is inference.

That is the entire confirmed record. Everything below is unconfirmed and logged for
completeness.

## Sighting Reports (Unconfirmed)

*Filed as received. Verified: none.*

- **SR-01 — The Posture.** Sakura's stance at the Dusk Vigil differs from her stance toward
  any known creature: lower, stiller, ears forward, no bark. (Most-cited report.)
- **SR-02 — The Cold Spot.** A patch near the frontier reported inexplicably cool. Posture
  change observed on crossing. (See `incident-archive.md`, INC-026.)
- **SR-03 — The Tracks.** Unfamiliar prints at the Far Corner after rain — not matching any
  catalogued creature. Gone before re-examination. The photo is blurry. (INC-027.)
- **SR-04 — The Movement.** Repeated reports of motion at the corner with no creature found.
  Wind? Probably. *Probably.*
- **SR-05 — The Quiet.** Other creatures reportedly avoid the deepest part of the Unknown
  Regions. The birds do not perch there. This is either meaningful or meaningless.
- **SR-06 — The Hour.** Reports cluster at dusk and, rarely, just before dawn. Never at midday.

## Contradictory Evidence

*Why the case never closes — in either direction.*

| For the vorg | Against the vorg |
|---|---|
| Sakura's restraint is real and uncharacteristic | No human has ever seen one |
| Unfamiliar tracks were found | The tracks were never re-found, the photo is useless |
| Other creatures avoid the corner | Creatures avoid lots of places; that's just shade |
| The behavior is consistent across years | Consistent behavior could be a learned habit, not a creature |
| Something passes through at unwatched hours | "Unwatched hours" means, by definition, no evidence |

The evidence is perfectly balanced. This is not an accident of the record. It is the *shape*
of the record, and it must be preserved.

## Rumors (Unsourced)

- That the vorg has always been here, and the yard was built around it.
- That there is not one vorg but a *line* of them, passing through the corner on business of
  their own.
- That Old Greycoat the squirrel knows what they are, and that this is why Sakura lets him be.
- That the Firefly Court at the edge of the Unknown Regions are not insects at all, but a
  *signal* — a light left on for something.
- That the vorg is not dangerous. That the vigil is not a guard. That Sakura is not holding
  the line *against* the corner, but *waiting* at it.

None of these are true. None of these are false. All of these are filed.

## The Sketches (Described)

*No photograph has ever resolved. The realm relies on sketches. None agree.*

- **Sketch A** — Low, four-legged, larger than a cat, indistinct. Drawn from the tracks.
- **Sketch B** — Tall and thin, barely there, drawn from "the movement." Resembles, the
  skeptic notes, a swaying branch.
- **Sketch C** — Nothing at all. A blank page labeled "the corner at dusk." Submitted, with
  total sincerity, as the most accurate sketch on file.

Sketch C is widely considered the best evidence.

## Implications for Gameplay

- The vorg threat level stays at **?** in all modes until the *family* confirms it canonically.
- The Unknown Regions cannot be fully mapped while the vorg is unresolved. Keep the corner
  dark.
- Vorg-adjacent content (the Dusk Vigil, the Cold Spot, the Tracks) should reward *curiosity*
  and *evidence-gathering*, never *resolution.* Players collect the mystery; they do not
  solve it.
- When confirmed (someday, by the real family), the vorg will almost certainly anchor its own
  game mode — a frontier expansion. Build toward the door without opening it.
- Every season, every expansion may add **one** new piece of vorg evidence. Never a
  conclusion. One stone at a time, forever.
