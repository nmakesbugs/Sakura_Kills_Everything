## Biography

Sakura was not appointed Warden of the Perimeter. There was no ceremony. There was
no vote. One day the backyard simply had a guardian, and she has held the post ever
since with a devotion that no one requested and no one has been able to revoke.

Her origin, as the family tells it, is unremarkable in every way except the one that
matters: she arrived, she looked at the yard, and she understood — instantly, totally,
and incorrectly — that she was now responsible for everything in it. From that moment
the squirrels became a problem to be managed, the birds became airspace to be
monitored, the rabbits became a developing situation, and the far corner became a
mystery she has never stopped circling.

She has never been off duty. She has slept on duty. She has eaten on duty. She has
been carried inside, mid-patrol, in full protest, and resumed the exact same patrol
the instant the door reopened, as though the interruption had never happened and time
itself had paused to wait for her.

She has held this post for years. In all that time she has caught one bird. She regards
this as proof the system works.

## Personality

Sakura is **sincere to a degree that reshapes the world around her.** She does not
perform enthusiasm. She has no irony in her body. When she believes a squirrel is a
threat to backyard order, she believes it the way other beings believe in gravity.

She is **joyful.** The hunt is not a burden to her. It is a calling, a privilege, and
the best part of every single day, every single day, forever. The ten-thousandth
squirrel is as thrilling as the first. She has no concept of "again." Everything is now.

She is **confident past the point of evidence.** A lifetime of unsuccessful squirrel
pursuit has not introduced a single grain of doubt. Each new chase is, in her mind, the
one. She is always certain. She is frequently incorrect. She is never discouraged.

She is **gentle where it counts.** For all the language of warfare and surveillance that
surrounds her, she has never once been cruel. The drama is the family's. The
seriousness is the narrator's. Sakura herself is simply a very good dog having the time
of her life, who would like the squirrel to come back so they can do it again.

## Strengths

- **Unbreakable morale.** Cannot be demoralized. The concept does not reach her.
- **Total commitment.** When she engages, she engages completely. No half-patrols.
- **Pattern recognition (selective).** Knows the fence line like a general knows a
  border. Knows exactly which window has the best yard view at each hour.
- **The Bark.** A signature alert vocalization. Family members can identify the target
  species — and sometimes the specific squirrel — purely from the bark pattern.
- **Endurance.** Will patrol until physically removed. Has out-waited weather.
- **Charisma.** Universally beloved. Has never lost a popularity contest because no one
  has ever been brave enough to run against her.

---

## Weaknesses

- **Object permanence is negotiable.** A squirrel that goes up a tree has, in a real
  sense, ceased to exist and also must be barked at forever.
- **The garden smells too interesting.** Tactical focus collapses near the Garden
  Sector. She has abandoned active pursuits to investigate a single compelling leaf.
- **Treats.** A documented and total vulnerability. The perimeter can be left
  completely undefended for the price of one (1) treat. Adversaries have not yet
  weaponized this. The family lives in quiet fear of the day they figure it out.
- **Cannot catch squirrels.** Not "rarely." Not "struggles to." Cannot. The record is
  perfect and it is perfectly zero.
- **Believes she is larger than she is.** Has attempted to assert dominance over animals,
  weather systems, and at least one lawnmower.

---

## Motivations

1. **Order.** The backyard must be kept correct. "Correct" is defined moment to moment
   by Sakura and is not available for review.
2. **The Squirrel.** Not squirrels in general — though them too — but the eternal,
   shapeshifting, singular Squirrel that has eluded her for her entire career. To catch
   it would complete her. She does not want to complete. She wants to chase.
3. **The Family.** Everything she does, she believes she does for them. The patrols are
   protection. The barking is reporting. The rolling in things is, somehow, also for them.
4. **Joy itself.** Beneath the doctrine, Sakura is motivated by the simplest thing in the
   world: it is fun, and she loves it, and she gets to do it again tomorrow.

---

## Fears

- **The vacuum.** A confirmed apex threat. Sakura's only acknowledged superior.
- **The far corner at dusk.** She monitors The Unknown Regions but does not fully commit.
  Something about it gives her pause. This is the strongest single piece of evidence for
  the vorg.
- **Being left out.** Not of danger — of *anything*. A closed door is a personal crisis.
- **The bath.** Tactically retreats. Has never won. Continues to resist on principle.

She does not fear: squirrels, birds, rabbits, larger dogs, the dark (mostly), thunder
(she has opinions about thunder but does not fear it — she *challenges* it).

---

## Aspirations

- To catch the Squirrel. Once. She would settle for once. She would not actually settle
  for once; she would immediately want the next one. But she believes she would settle.
- To map The Unknown Regions. Someday. When she is ready. She is not yet ready.
- To be recognized — though she already is, completely, by everyone — as the Warden.
- To be present for everything that ever happens. Forever. With no exceptions.

## Daily Routine

*A typical operational day, as reconstructed from observed behavior.*

- **0600 — Dawn Patrol.** First perimeter sweep. Establishes that the yard survived the
  night. It always has. She is always relieved and surprised.
- **0700 — The Window Watch.** If denied yard access, assumes the interior observation
  post with the superior sightline. Monitors. Reports (loudly).
- **0900 — First Engagement.** Statistically the most likely squirrel contact of the day.
  Outcome: the squirrel goes up the fence. The open field is declared secured.
- **1100 — Garden Inspection.** Investigates the Garden Sector. The rabbits are not there.
  They were recently there. This is noted with great concern.
- **1300 — Strategic Rest.** Sleeps. On duty. One ear remains deployed.
- **1500 — Afternoon Patrol.** Repeat of dawn patrol with renewed and total enthusiasm,
  as though it is the first patrol that has ever occurred in history.
- **1730 — The Dusk Vigil.** Faces The Unknown Regions. Holds. Does not commit. Returns.
- **2000 — Stand Down (Disputed).** Comes inside. Considers herself still technically on
  duty. Will respond to any sound. Any sound at all.

## Famous Victories

- **The Bird Incident.** The single confirmed catch. The cornerstone of her legend. One
  bird, intercepted clean out of the air. (Full account: `sakura-canon-bible.md` and
  `incident-archive.md`.) She does not discuss it. The family does. Constantly.
- **The Securing of the Open Field.** Has "secured" the open field thousands of times.
  Each securing is total and each is immediately undone by the next squirrel. The
  victories still count. She insists they count.
- **The Lawnmower Standoff.** Did not defeat the lawnmower. Did not retreat from the
  lawnmower. The result is recorded as a draw and she walks away from it like a champion.
- **Every Single Patrol.** Each completed patrol is, by her doctrine, a victory, because
  nothing got worse on her watch. By this standard she is undefeated across years.

---

## Famous Failures

*Reframed, per canon law, as moral victories. The narrator finds the win.*

- **The Squirrel, In Its Entirety.** A perfect, career-spanning record of zero. Officially
  logged as "ongoing operation." It is not a failure. It is a relationship.
- **The Fence Line Highway.** Despite continuous patrol, the squirrels continue to use the
  fence as a highway. The patrol has had no deterrent effect whatsoever. Sakura considers
  the patrol essential anyway, and she is, in a sense impossible to argue with, correct.
- **The Garden Distraction.** Has lost more pursuits to an interesting smell than to any
  living adversary. The smells remain undefeated.

## Personal Myths

*Stories Sakura appears to believe about herself. Their truth value is not the point.*

- That she has personally prevented countless squirrel incursions. (Unverifiable. She is
  certain. The incursions did not happen, which she regards as proof.)
- That the family's safety depends, daily, on her vigilance.
- That she once nearly caught The Squirrel and was robbed by circumstance. (This is every
  chase. She remembers each as the close one.)
- That she understands the vorg situation better than anyone and is simply choosing not
  to act yet.

---

## Things She Believes That Are Obviously Untrue

- That she can catch a squirrel. (She cannot. The record is flawless.)
- That a squirrel up a tree will, given enough barking, come back down to face her.
- That the mail carrier is repelled each day by her efforts, rather than by having
  finished delivering the mail.
- That she is approximately the size of a wolf.
- That the vacuum can be reasoned with.
- That she is, at any given moment, hiding.
- That the bird was the beginning of a streak.

## The Heart of the Character

Strip away the dossier language, the threat tiers, the surveillance vocabulary, and what
remains is very simple, and it is the most important thing in this entire document:

**Sakura is a good dog who loves her family, loves her yard, and loves her job, and who
gets to wake up every morning and do the thing she loves most, surrounded by the people
she loves most, having completely forgotten that yesterday's squirrel got away.**

Everything else in this world is built on top of that. Never lose it.
