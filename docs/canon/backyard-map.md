# Backyard Map — The Canonical Geography

> Tier 1 canon. The backyard is not a yard. It is a contested wilderness with named
> territories, legendary sites, patrol sectors, and at least one location no one will
> map. What follows is the explorer's record of a kingdom roughly the size of a yard.
>
> Distances are measured in Sakura-lengths and in dread. Boundaries are behavioral, not
> physical. The map updates as new incidents provide data.

## I. The Heartlands (Near the House)

### 1. The Great Patio
**Region:** Adjacent to the house. The seat of power.
**Terrain:** Hardscape, furniture, potted plants, the warm stones.
**Common encounters:** Bold birds, exploratory bugs, the occasional reconnaissance rabbit.
**History:** Every campaign in recorded history has begun here. The patio is where Sakura
surveys her domain before committing, where victories are celebrated, and where defeats are
re-classified as victories.
**Significance:** The throne room. To hold the patio is to hold the yard. It has never
fallen.
**Related stories:** *The Morning Survey* (daily); *The Great Patio Standoff*.

### 2. The Threshold
**Region:** The door, and the sacred ground immediately before it.
**Terrain:** The boundary between Command (indoors) and the Theater (outdoors).
**Common encounters:** The family. Anticipation. The single most important event of any
day: the door opening.
**History:** The Threshold is where Sakura waits, where she is carried across mid-protest,
and where she resumes any interrupted patrol with zero loss of momentum.
**Significance:** The most strategically vital point in the realm. Whoever controls the
Threshold controls the war. The family controls the Threshold. Sakura is working on it.

### 3. The Window Posts
**Region:** Interior observation positions overlooking the yard.
**Terrain:** Sills, couch-backs, the good chair.
**Common encounters:** Everything, at a distance, infuriatingly out of reach.
**History:** When denied yard access, Sakura assumes the Post with the superior sightline
for the hour and conducts surveillance through the glass.
**Significance:** Forward operating bases of the indoor campaign. The barking carries.

### 4. The Hose Coil
**Region:** Patio edge.
**Terrain:** A coiled green serpent that has never moved and is watched closely anyway.
**Common encounters:** None. That is the suspicious part.
**History:** Sakura has classified the hose as dormant rather than harmless. The
distinction matters to her.
**Significance:** A monument to vigilance against threats that do not exist, which are the
only threats one can never fail to stop.

## II. The Open Field (The Central Plain)

### 5. The Open Field
**Region:** The grassy center of the realm.
**Terrain:** Open grass. No cover for prey. Speed country.
**Common encounters:** Squirrels in transit; bird flyovers; the great chases.
**History:** The site of nearly every "almost." More near-victories have occurred here
than anywhere else in the world. The field has witnessed thousands of securings.
**Significance:** Sakura's preferred battleground — the only place her speed is an
advantage and cover cannot save the enemy. The squirrels know this and cross it quickly.
**Related stories:** *The Securing of the Open Field* (ongoing, thousands of times).

### 6. The Center Stone
**Region:** Mid-field.
**Terrain:** A single notable stone (or stepping-stone) at the field's heart.
**Common encounters:** A resting Sakura; the occasional sunbather bird that has not read
the threat assessment.
**Significance:** The realm's symbolic center. Sakura naps here, on duty, with one ear
deployed, like a knight asleep at the exact middle of the kingdom.

### 7. The Sun Patch
**Region:** A migrating territory of warmth that moves across the field with the day.
**Terrain:** Wherever the light is best.
**History:** The only territory in the realm that Sakura pursues purely for pleasure. She
tracks it across the yard with more consistency than she tracks any squirrel.
**Significance:** Proof that even the Warden takes leave. The Sun Patch is undefeated and
unopposed.

### 8. The Bald Spot
**Region:** A worn patch of grass, off-center.
**Terrain:** Grass that has given its life to the cause.
**History:** Created by the cumulative force of ten thousand sudden stops, pivots, and
launches. A scar of pure enthusiasm.
**Significance:** The yard's own service medal. Physical evidence of a career.

## III. The Eastern Territories (The Fence)

### 9. The Great Fence Line
**Region:** The far eastern boundary.
**Terrain:** Grass, fence posts, exterior shrub cover.
**Common encounters:** Squirrels. Always squirrels. The highest creature traffic in the realm.
**History:** The single most contested feature of the entire kingdom. The squirrels use
the fence as a highway; Sakura patrols it without ceasing; the patrol has had no measurable
deterrent effect across the entire span of her career. She patrols it anyway, and she is —
infuriatingly, unanswerably — correct to.
**Significance:** To control the fence is to control squirrel access to the realm. No one
controls the fence. The fence belongs to the squirrels. Sakura disputes this daily.
**Related stories:** *The Fence Line Highway*; *The Patrol That Changed Nothing*.

### 10. The Knothole Gate
**Region:** A specific gap or knothole in the fence.
**Terrain:** One small hole with enormous strategic consequences.
**Common encounters:** Squirrels, entering and exiting the realm with documents Sakura
does not recognize.
**History:** The primary point of unauthorized entry. Sakura has stationed herself before
it. The squirrels simply use a different gap. There is always a different gap.
**Significance:** The realm's porous border made visible. A lesson in the limits of power.

### 11. The Corner Post
**Region:** Where the eastern fence meets the northern boundary.
**Terrain:** A tall post; a vantage; a frontier marker.
**Common encounters:** A lookout squirrel, allegedly. Sakura believes one squirrel is
*always* posted here as a sentry. She may be right.
**Significance:** The eastern command point. Closest fortified position to the Unknown
Regions. Sakura's patrols slow here.

### 12. The Diplomatic Fence (The Cat's Border)
**Region:** A stretch of fence bordering the neighbor's domain.
**Terrain:** Standard fence; abnormal tension.
**Common encounters:** **The Neighbor's Cat.** Classification disputed. Status: cold peace.
**History:** Site of an unresolved diplomatic incident. Neither party has crossed. Neither
party has stood down. The two regard each other through the slats with the dignity of rival
heads of state who will never, ever admit the other exists.
**Significance:** The realm's only active foreign border. See `organizations.md`.

## IV. The Garden Frontier (Rabbit Country)

### 13. The Garden Frontier
**Region:** The mid-left territory; raised beds and cultivated ground.
**Terrain:** Raised beds, mulch, low cover, and smells of devastating tactical interest.
**Common encounters:** Rabbits. The rabbits are not there. The rabbits were *recently*
there. This is, somehow, worse.
**History:** Contested ground where two doctrines collide: Sakura's need to patrol and the
garden's overwhelming need to be sniffed. Pursuits collapse here with regularity.
**Significance:** The front line of the Rabbit Question. Where the war meets the nose, and
the nose usually wins.
**Related stories:** *The Garden Distraction*; *The Inspection That Found Nothing*.

### 14. Warlord's Hollow
**Region:** A shadowed gap beneath the largest garden bed.
**Terrain:** A burrow-shaped darkness. Cover. Cold command.
**Common encounters:** The rabbit known as **General Thistle**, allegedly. (See catalog.)
**History:** Believed to be the rabbit seat of power within the realm. Sakura has stared
into it. It has stared back. Nothing has come of the staring, which is itself ominous.
**Significance:** The presumed capital of the Rabbit Coalition inside the wire.

### 15. The Tomato Cathedral
**Region:** The tallest staked plants in the garden.
**Terrain:** A green vaulted height; dappled light; the smell of summer.
**Common encounters:** Hovering insects; one extremely confident bird.
**History:** In high summer this becomes the most fragrant and distracting site in the
realm. Sakura has abandoned more than one pursuit upon entering it, struck motionless by
the sheer information of the place.
**Significance:** Sacred ground to the nose. A seasonal hazard to focus. See
`seasonal-world.md`.

### 16. The Mulch Flats
**Region:** Open cultivated ground at the garden's edge.
**Terrain:** Soft, diggable, recently disturbed.
**Common encounters:** Evidence. Tracks. The aftermath of rabbits.
**Significance:** The realm's premier site for the discovery of *signs* — paw prints,
disturbances, clues. Where investigations begin. See `incident-archive.md`.

## V. The Tree Line (The Canopy Frontier)

### 17. The Tree Line
**Region:** The wooded boundary; the vertical frontier.
**Terrain:** Trunks, roots, and the great unreachable above.
**Common encounters:** Squirrels ascending to safety; birds staging; the laws of physics,
which favor the enemy here.
**History:** The realm's great vertical limit — the place where pursuit ends and barking
begins. A squirrel that reaches the Tree Line has, by ancient and bitter convention, won.
**Significance:** The ceiling of the war. Sakura's authority is total on the ground and
ends, absolutely, at the lowest branch.

### 18. The Old Tree (The Sentinel)
**Region:** The largest tree on the line.
**Terrain:** A broad trunk, a high canopy, deep roots.
**Common encounters:** The squirrel high command, allegedly, holding court in the upper
branches.
**History:** The single most significant vertical feature in the realm. Believed by the
family to be the squirrels' true capital — beyond reach, beyond patrol, beyond hope. Sakura
has circled its base more times than any other spot on earth.
**Significance:** The unconquerable fortress. The Tree the war is, in the end, about.
**Related stories:** *The Siege of the Old Tree* (permanent, unwinnable, beloved).

### 19. The Root Throne
**Region:** The exposed roots at the base of the Old Tree.
**Terrain:** A natural seat of gnarled wood.
**History:** Where Sakura sits and looks up. For a long time. Every day. Considering.
**Significance:** The most poignant location in the realm — the spot where the Warden
confronts the one limit she cannot patrol her way past, and remains, somehow, undiscouraged.

### 20. The Drop Zone
**Region:** Ground beneath the canopy where things fall.
**Terrain:** Littered with acorns, twigs, and aerial debris.
**Common encounters:** Falling acorns, regarded by Sakura as either gifts or provocations
depending on the day. Possibly both.
**History:** The squirrels drop things from above. Whether this is bombardment, taunting,
or simple carelessness is a matter of ongoing family debate.
**Significance:** The only theater where the enemy holds the high ground *and* the artillery.

## VI. The Unknown Regions (Here Be Vorgs)

### 21. The Unknown Regions
**Region:** The far back corner, at maximum range from the house.
**Terrain:** Unknown. Overgrown, possibly. Mapped, never.
**Common encounters:** Unconfirmed. Movement. The feeling of being regarded.
**History:** Sakura monitors this zone and does not fully commit to it. This single,
consistent, uncharacteristic restraint is the strongest evidence in existence for the vorg.
The Warden fears nothing — and yet she holds, here, at dusk, every day.
**Significance:** The realm's great mystery and its dark frontier. It will not be mapped
until canon allows. See `vorg-mythology.md`.

### 22. The Dusk Vigil Point
**Region:** The forward edge of the known yard, facing the Unknown Regions.
**Terrain:** The last lit ground before the uncharted dark.
**History:** Where Sakura faces the far corner each evening, holds the line, does not
advance, and returns. The most disciplined act of her entire career — and she is not a
disciplined dog.
**Significance:** The border between the known world and the mystery. Sacred. Watched.

### 23. The Cold Spot
**Region:** Somewhere within or before the Unknown Regions.
**Terrain:** A patch reported to be inexplicably cooler. Unverified.
**Common encounters:** None confirmed. A change in Sakura's posture. A theory of Cias's.
**Significance:** Circumstantial vorg evidence. Logged for completeness. Not resolved.

### 24. The Far Fence Corner
**Region:** The exact rearmost point of the realm.
**Terrain:** Where two fences meet at the kingdom's end.
**History:** The literal edge of the mapped world. Beyond it: the neighbor's, the alley,
the rumored wider realms — and whatever passes through the corner at hours no one watches.
**Significance:** Land's end. The frame of the entire mythology.

## VII. Legendary & Mystery Sites

### 25. The Site of the Bird Incident
**Region:** Location held in confidence by family consensus; somewhere in the open ground.
**Terrain:** Ordinary grass, made sacred.
**History:** Here occurred the single confirmed catch of Sakura's career — one bird, taken
from the air. (See `sakura-canon-bible.md`, `incident-archive.md`.) The exact spot is
treated with reverence and a little disbelief.
**Significance:** The holiest ground in the realm. The proof that it is all, sometimes,
real. Birds must die. (One did.)

### 26. The Vanishing Path
**Region:** A route between the Garden Frontier and the Tree Line that creatures use to
disappear.
**Terrain:** A corridor of cover; a trick of sightlines.
**History:** Prey entering the path are reliably lost. Sakura has never successfully
followed anything through it. It is believed to bend in ways the eye cannot follow.
**Significance:** The realm's great unsolved tactical puzzle.

### 27. The Whispering Gutter
**Region:** Along the house's edge; the downspout and its runoff channel.
**Terrain:** Metal, echo, and the sound of moving water after rain.
**History:** Makes noises Sakura investigates and the family cannot explain. Probably water.
Probably.
**Significance:** A minor mystery kept alive on purpose, because a realm should have a few.

### 28. The Forgotten Toy Field
**Region:** Scattered ground where toys are lost and slowly reclaimed by the yard.
**Terrain:** Tall grass; buried treasure; archaeology.
**History:** Sakura periodically unearths a toy presumed lost to history and is, each time,
completely and newly overjoyed.
**Significance:** A site of pure happiness with no strategic value whatsoever, which makes
it one of the most important places in the entire kingdom.

## Patrol Sectors (Operational Overlay)

For gameplay, the realm divides into four patrol sectors, each with its own rhythm:

| Sector | Territories | Primary threat | Patrol mood |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Sector I — The Heartlands** | Patio, Threshold, Windows | Bold birds, recon rabbits | Confident |
| **Sector II — The Plain** | Open Field, Center Stone | Squirrels in transit | Eager |
| **Sector III — The Borders** | Fence Line, Garden, Tree Line | Squirrels, rabbits | Contested |
| **Sector IV — The Frontier** | Unknown Regions, Far Corner | Unknown (?) | Wary |

## Map Law

- This map reflects **Sakura's understanding of the yard,** not a surveyor's.
- Locations may be promoted, renamed, or revealed as new incidents are logged.
- The Unknown Regions remain deliberately incomplete. Do not map them until canon allows.
- Every new game mode should be able to find a home somewhere on this map. There is room.
- A child should want to explore it. An adult should smile at the names. Both should
  believe, just a little, that the far corner is worth being careful about.
