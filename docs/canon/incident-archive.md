# The Incident Archive

> Tier 1 canon. The collected history of the backyard, one incident at a time. Each entry
> records what was reported, what the realm *officially* concluded, and — quietly, in a
> separate line — what most likely actually happened. The gap between the two is the soul
> of this entire project.
>
> These incidents are the raw material of gameplay: quests, achievements, events, and
> seasonal content all grow from here. New incidents are added as they occur and are
> verified by family consensus. **An event is not canon until the Witness confirms it.**

---

## How to Read an Entry

- **Date classification** — When it happened, in the realm's loose chronology.
- **Summary** — The event as it entered the record.
- **Witness account** — Who saw it, and what they reported.
- **Official interpretation** — What the realm declared it to mean. (Earnest. Dramatic.)
- **Likely reality** — What probably happened. (Affectionate. Usually simpler.)

The **Official interpretation** is the legend. The **Likely reality** is the truth. Both
are canon. The game lives in the space between them.

## ERA I — THE FOUNDING (Earliest Records)

### INC-001 · The Bird Incident ⭐ FOUNDATIONAL
**Date:** Undated. The cornerstone legend.
**Summary:** Sakura was discovered with blood on her nose. Investigation revealed a bird,
deceased. She had caught it from the air, thrashed it, and rolled in it, deeply pleased.
**Witness account:** Tanisha — stepped outside, noticed the nose, found the bird, understood
that history had occurred.
**Official interpretation:** The single confirmed aerial intercept in the realm's history.
Sakura's greatest achievement. Definitive proof that the impossible is, on rare days,
possible. Birds must die. (One did.)
**Likely reality:** Exactly what it appears to be — a once-in-a-career convergence of luck,
timing, and total commitment. It really happened. That is what makes it sacred.

### INC-002 · The Appointment of the Warden
**Date:** The Founding. Day One.
**Summary:** Sakura arrived, surveyed the yard, and assumed total responsibility for it.
**Witness account:** The whole family. No one remembers granting authority. The authority
simply existed by evening.
**Official interpretation:** The realm gained its eternal guardian. A post was created and
filled in the same instant. The age of order began.
**Likely reality:** A new dog explored a new yard and liked it. Everything else is the
story the family built on top of one happy afternoon.

### INC-003 · The First Patrol
**Date:** The Founding, shortly after.
**Summary:** Sakura conducted the realm's first perimeter sweep, establishing the route
still walked today.
**Witness account:** Nick, who opened the door and thereby — unknowingly — began everything.
**Official interpretation:** The patrol doctrine was established. The fence line was claimed.
The squirrels were put on notice.
**Likely reality:** The squirrels were not put on notice. The squirrels did not notice. The
route, however, has genuinely never changed, which is its own small miracle.

## ERA II — THE SQUIRREL WARS (Ongoing)

### INC-004 · The Securing of the Open Field
**Date:** Recurring. Thousands of instances.
**Summary:** A squirrel crossed the field; Sakura pursued; the squirrel reached the fence;
Sakura declared the field secured.
**Witness account:** Everyone, constantly.
**Official interpretation:** A decisive territorial victory. The open field, repeatedly,
held.
**Likely reality:** The squirrel was never in danger and the field was never threatened. But
nothing got worse on her watch, so by her own ironclad logic, she won. She is hard to argue
with.

### INC-005 · The Fence Line Highway
**Date:** Ongoing, daily.
**Summary:** Despite continuous patrol, squirrels continue to use the fence as a thoroughfare.
**Witness account:** Soren, who has the data and is not happy about it.
**Official interpretation:** The patrol remains essential to *contain* the highway, which
would otherwise be far worse.
**Likely reality:** The patrol has zero deterrent effect. The highway is exactly as busy as
it would be without her. And yet — Soren cannot prove the yard would not be worse, so the
patrol stands, undefeated by logic.

### INC-006 · The Acorn Near-Catch
**Date:** A bright afternoon, mid-era.
**Summary:** Sakura came within a single body-length of catching Acorn the squirrel on the
open field. The closest non-bird contact ever recorded.
**Witness account:** Nolyn, who screamed (encouragingly).
**Official interpretation:** Proof the system works. The Squirrel can be caught. It is only
a matter of time.
**Likely reality:** Acorn misjudged his route and got lucky. He tells it as a daring escape.
Both versions are, by now, equally true.

### INC-007 · The Siege of the Old Tree
**Date:** Permanent.
**Summary:** Sakura maintains an unending siege on the Old Tree, where the squirrel high
command allegedly resides.
**Witness account:** All. She is always, on some level, besieging the tree.
**Official interpretation:** Strategic containment of enemy command. Field Marshal Bramble
is pinned in his fortress.
**Likely reality:** It is a tree. Squirrels live in it. They come and go freely above her.
The siege is a feeling, not a fact, and it is a wonderful feeling.

### INC-008 · The Sentry's Alarm
**Date:** Recurring.
**Summary:** Each time Sakura commits to the field, an alarm chitter empties it instantly.
**Witness account:** Cias, who insists there is a posted lookout. (There is.)
**Official interpretation:** Enemy counter-intelligence. The Corner Sentry must be
neutralized to enable a clean operation.
**Likely reality:** Squirrels warn each other. It is not personal. Sakura has decided it is
extremely personal.

### INC-009 · Nibbles on the Patio
**Date:** A morning of infamy.
**Summary:** Nibbles the Bold entered the Great Patio itself — the seat of power — and stole
in plain sight.
**Witness account:** Tanisha, from the window, narrating in real time.
**Official interpretation:** A constitutional crisis. An attack on the throne room. Security
posture permanently elevated.
**Likely reality:** A hungry squirrel found food near the house. It went very well for him.
He has been back. It keeps going well for him.

### INC-010 · The Truce of Old Greycoat
**Date:** Quiet, undated.
**Summary:** Sakura ceased pursuing the ancient squirrel Old Greycoat. No one knows when or
why.
**Witness account:** Noticed gradually by the whole family; confirmed by its own absence of
drama.
**Official interpretation:** A battlefield understanding between two veterans of a long war.
**Likely reality:** The old squirrel stopped running, and something in Sakura — who chases
*motion* — simply let him be. The realm's most unexpectedly tender fact.

## ERA III — THE RABBIT QUESTION

### INC-011 · The Inspection That Found Nothing
**Date:** Recurring.
**Summary:** Sakura inspected the Garden Frontier. The rabbits were not there. They had been
there *recently.*
**Witness account:** Everyone who has ever watched her sniff a warm, empty hollow.
**Official interpretation:** Near-miss interdiction. The enemy fled moments before contact.
Pressure is working.
**Likely reality:** The rabbits leave before she arrives, every time, easily. "Recently
there" is the closest she gets and she treats it as a clue rather than a defeat.

### INC-012 · The Staring of General Thistle
**Date:** Multiple confrontations.
**Summary:** Sakura and the rabbit warlord General Thistle held a prolonged, motionless
stare across the mulch. Nothing followed.
**Witness account:** Soren, who timed it and was unsettled by the length.
**Official interpretation:** A tense diplomatic standoff between rival powers. A war of nerve.
**Likely reality:** A dog and a rabbit looked at each other for a while. The rabbit won by
not moving. The intensity is entirely real and entirely about nothing.

### INC-013 · The Garden Distraction
**Date:** Recurring, worst in summer.
**Summary:** Sakura abandoned an active rabbit pursuit to investigate a single compelling
smell.
**Witness account:** Nick, who watched a sure thing dissolve into sniffing.
**Official interpretation:** Tactical reconnaissance. The scent contained vital intelligence.
**Likely reality:** The garden smells incredible and her nose is in charge. The smells remain
the realm's most reliable undefeated power.

### INC-014 · The Phantom's Sign
**Date:** A still morning.
**Summary:** A garden bed was found disturbed, a hollow still warm, prints leading to nowhere.
No rabbit seen.
**Witness account:** Cias, who declared it the work of the Phantom of the Garden.
**Official interpretation:** Evidence of an unseen rabbit power operating with advanced
stealth.
**Likely reality:** A rabbit left right before anyone looked. Probably Thistle. The "leading
nowhere" is just where the family stopped following. The mystery is better than the answer.

### INC-015 · The Kit Encounter
**Date:** A spring afternoon.
**Summary:** Sakura cornered a group of baby rabbits (Kits) and then visibly did not know
what to do.
**Witness account:** Tanisha, who melted, on the record.
**Official interpretation:** Mercy extended by the Warden in accordance with the unwritten
codes of backyard war.
**Likely reality:** They were too small and round and she is, beneath the doctrine, a soft
and gentle dog. A documented crack in the hard line, and the family loves her more for it.

## ERA IV — THE SKIES

### INC-016 · Captain Flit's Patio Violations
**Date:** Daily.
**Summary:** The robin Captain Flit lands just out of reach on the patio, repeatedly, with
perfect timing.
**Witness account:** All. It is maddening to watch.
**Official interpretation:** Deliberate airspace provocation by a known repeat offender.
Surveillance ongoing.
**Likely reality:** A robin found a safe, food-adjacent spot and uses it. He is not taunting
her. It only looks *exactly* like taunting. (It might be a little bit taunting.)

### INC-017 · The Hush
**Date:** A rare overcast afternoon.
**Summary:** Every creature in the yard went silent and still at once. Sakura watched the sky
without barking. A shadow may have crossed the field.
**Witness account:** Nolyn, who for once went quiet too.
**Official interpretation:** The passage of an apex aerial power. The realm acknowledged a
superior and held its breath.
**Likely reality:** A hawk, probably, passed overhead. The silence was real. The one time the
whole yard agreed on something, and it was fear, and then it passed.

### INC-018 · The Sparrow Airfield Dispute
**Date:** Recurring around the feeder.
**Summary:** A sparrow flock claimed the feeder zone, scattering and reforming faster than
Sakura could respond.
**Witness account:** The family, amused.
**Official interpretation:** A contested airfield held by superior numbers and coordination.
**Likely reality:** Small fast birds are small and fast. The "coordination" is just birds
being birds. Sakura experiences it as organized warfare.

### INC-019 · The Crow's Survey
**Date:** Occasional.
**Summary:** A large crow visited, perched, watched the yard at length, and departed
unhurried.
**Witness account:** Cias, who is convinced it was *reporting* to someone.
**Official interpretation:** Reconnaissance by an agent of an outside power (the Crow
Council). The yard is being observed from beyond the fence.
**Likely reality:** A crow looked at a yard. Crows do that. But it really did seem like it
*knew* something, and that feeling is now permanent canon.

## ERA V — THE DOMESTIC FRONT

### INC-020 · The Lawnmower Standoff
**Date:** Each mowing.
**Summary:** Sakura confronted the lawnmower. Neither retreated. Neither prevailed.
**Witness account:** Nick, operating the lawnmower.
**Official interpretation:** An honorable draw against a larger, louder adversary. Walked away
from like a champion.
**Likely reality:** She barked at a machine and was brought inside. The "draw" is generous.
She earned it anyway.

### INC-021 · The Vacuum Incident
**Date:** Whenever the vacuum appears.
**Summary:** The realm's only acknowledged apex threat activated indoors. Sakura conducted a
tactical withdrawal.
**Witness account:** All, sympathetically.
**Official interpretation:** Strategic repositioning in the face of an overwhelming and
possibly unkillable enemy.
**Likely reality:** She does not like the vacuum. Few do. The one creature in the entire
mythology she will openly flee. We do not judge her for it.

### INC-022 · The Bath Resistance
**Date:** Periodic.
**Summary:** Sakura resisted, fled, and ultimately submitted to the bath, having lost without
ever conceding.
**Witness account:** Tanisha, damp.
**Official interpretation:** A principled stand against an unjust process. Honor maintained in
defeat.
**Likely reality:** She does not want the bath, has never wanted the bath, and will resist the
bath forever on pure principle despite a flawless record of losing.

### INC-023 · The Door That Closed
**Date:** Recurring; emotionally significant.
**Summary:** A door closed with Sakura on the wrong side of the family.
**Witness account:** Everyone, through the door.
**Official interpretation:** A grave injustice and a temporary but total crisis of state.
**Likely reality:** A door closed for ordinary reasons for a short time. To Sakura it was the
end of the world, every time, freshly. Being left out of *anything* is her deepest fear.

### INC-024 · The Forgotten Toy, Recovered
**Date:** A joyful afternoon.
**Summary:** Sakura unearthed a toy long presumed lost to history and was overwhelmed with joy.
**Witness account:** Nolyn, who joined the celebration.
**Official interpretation:** Recovery of a lost artifact of immense historical value.
**Likely reality:** She found an old toy in the grass and was perfectly, completely happy. No
strategy. No enemy. Just joy. One of the most important entries in the archive.

## ERA VI — THE FRONTIER

### INC-025 · The Dusk Vigil
**Date:** Every evening.
**Summary:** Sakura faces the Unknown Regions at dusk, holds the line, does not advance, and
returns inside.
**Witness account:** Cias, who watches her watch the corner.
**Official interpretation:** Disciplined containment of an unconfirmed frontier threat. The
Warden guards the edge of the known world.
**Likely reality:** Unknown. This is the entry the archive cannot close. She — who fears
almost nothing — consistently will not commit to the far corner at dusk. No one knows why.
This single behavior is the strongest evidence for the vorg. See `vorg-mythology.md`.

### INC-026 · The Cold Spot Report
**Date:** Unverified.
**Summary:** A patch near the Unknown Regions was reported inexplicably cooler. Sakura's
posture changed crossing it.
**Witness account:** Cias (believer); disputed by Soren (skeptic).
**Official interpretation:** Possible environmental trace of vorg presence. Logged, not
confirmed.
**Likely reality:** Probably shade. Probably nothing. *Probably.* The archive keeps it open
on purpose.

### INC-027 · The Tracks at the Far Corner
**Date:** One morning after rain.
**Summary:** Unfamiliar tracks were found at the Far Fence Corner — not squirrel, not rabbit,
not cat. Gone by the time anyone returned with better eyes.
**Witness account:** Nolyn found them; Nick photographed them; the photo was, of course,
blurry.
**Official interpretation:** The first physical evidence of an unclassified frontier creature.
**Likely reality:** Unknown and unresolved. Could be anything. The family still argues about
the photo. The mystery grew that day, and it has not shrunk since.

## Archive Law

- Every entry carries **both** an Official interpretation and a Likely reality. Never drop the
  second line — the honesty *is* the warmth.
- The Witness (Tanisha) holds final authority on what enters the record.
- Real events outrank invented ones. When a true Sakura story arrives, it becomes a numbered
  incident and supersedes any guess.
- Frontier incidents (vorg-adjacent) should *deepen* the mystery, never resolve it.
- The archive is open-ended. A backyard civilization generates history forever. Keep writing
  it down.
