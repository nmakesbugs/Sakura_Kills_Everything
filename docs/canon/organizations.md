# Organizations of the Backyard

> Tier 1 canon. The realm is not a free-for-all. It is a tangle of factions, councils,
> authorities, and syndicates — each with a charter, a membership, and a position on Sakura.
> The war for the backyard is, properly understood, a war of *institutions.*
>
> None of these organizations are evil. Each is sincerely, ridiculously devoted to its own
> charter. The comedy is in the bureaucracy of a backyard that has decided it is a
> civilization.

---

## How Factions Work

Every creature in the `creature-catalog.md` belongs to — or defies — one of these powers.
Factions give the realm its politics: alliances, borders, rivalries, and the occasional
summit. In gameplay terms, they are the source of **campaigns, reputation systems,
diplomacy, and faction-flavored events.**

Sakura, notably, belongs to no faction. She *is* a faction. A faction of one, with a charter
no one else has read, recognized by every other power as a force of nature rather than a peer.

**Standing with Sakura** is rated: **War · Cold War · Rivalry · Wary Peace · Truce · Alliance.**

## 1. The Squirrel Directorate
**Also known as:** The Fence Syndicate · The High Branch
**Purpose:** Total freedom of squirrel movement across the realm; control of the fence-line
highway and the Old Tree.
**Membership:** All squirrels, allegedly under the distant command of Field Marshal Bramble.
Includes the Corner Sentry watch, the field-runners (Acorn), and the bold patio raiders
(Nibbles).
**History:** The realm's oldest and most powerful organization. Predates the Warden. Has
never lost a member to Sakura. Operates with the calm of a power that has simply never been
beaten.
**Standing with Sakura:** **War.** The defining conflict of the realm. Eternal, total, and —
crucially — bloodless. Zero confirmed casualties on either side.
**Goals:** Continue exactly as they are, forever, ideally while being chased, which they
appear to enjoy.

## 2. The Patio Authority
**Purpose:** Administration of the Heartlands; maintenance of order; official communications;
the formal denial of the vorg.
**Membership:** Notionally the realm's governing body. In practice: the family, the doves
(Coo & Roo) as permanent fixtures, and Sakura as enforcement. Issues statements no creature
obeys.
**History:** The realm's bureaucratic heart. Keeps the records, posts the threat levels, and
maintains — with increasingly suspicious firmness — that there is no vorg.
**Standing with Sakura:** **Alliance.** She is its sword. It is her paperwork.
**Goals:** Stability, continuity, and plausible deniability about the far corner.

## 3. The Rabbit Coalition
**Also known as:** The Garden Sovereignty
**Purpose:** Rabbit self-determination within the Garden Frontier; recognition of the garden
as sovereign rabbit territory.
**Membership:** General Thistle (warlord), Duchess Clover (diplomat), the Twitch (lieutenant),
the Kits, and the unseen Phantom. A coalition of soldiers and aristocrats who barely
cooperate.
**History:** Holds the garden through patience, stillness, and a genius for not being there
when Sakura arrives. The realm's most *territorial* power.
**Standing with Sakura:** **Cold War.** Long stares, no contact, a border that has never been
crossed by either side.
**Goals:** Keep the garden. Be presumed innocent (impossible; all rabbits are suspicious).

## 4. The Bird Council
**Also known as:** The Aerial Assembly
**Purpose:** Governance of all airspace above the realm; defense of the sacred principle that
the ground-bound shall not reach the sky.
**Membership:** Captain Flit (patio chair), the Sparrow Squadron (numbers), the Mourning Pair
(neutral elders). The Crow Council Envoy attends as a foreign observer.
**History:** Holds an absolute structural advantage — they can fly — and never lets anyone
forget it. Has lost exactly one member in the realm's entire history. They have not forgotten
who took him.
**Standing with Sakura:** **Rivalry**, sharpened by grief. The Bird Incident is, to the
Council, a foundational trauma. To Sakura, a triumph. To the birds, a reason to stay just out
of reach forever.
**Goals:** Maintain air supremacy. Avoid a second Incident at all costs.

## 5. The Crow Council
**Purpose:** Unknown. Operates from beyond the fence, across the wider neighborhood. Observes.
**Membership:** Crows. The Envoy is their only confirmed presence in the realm.
**History:** A foreign power of unsettling intelligence. Treats the backyard as one small
province of a much larger map it alone can see. Reports to whom, about what, remains unknown.
**Standing with Sakura:** **Wary Peace.** She watches the Envoy without barking — a rare
honor, or a rare caution.
**Goals:** Information. The Crow Council is the realm's reminder that there is a *world out
there,* and it is paying attention.

## 6. The Chipmunk Tunnel Authority
**Also known as:** The Underground
**Purpose:** Maintenance of the secret passages beneath the realm; movement of contraband
below the notice of all surface powers.
**Membership:** Pockets (smuggler), Stripe (surface agent), and a presumed network never
counted.
**History:** If real, the most successful organization in the realm — they move freely
*beneath* every patrol, undetected, indifferent to the war above. Evidence: holes that appear
overnight.
**Standing with Sakura:** **Cold War (subterranean).** She cannot fight what she cannot reach.
The mounds offend her deeply.
**Goals:** Keep the tunnels secret. Keep moving.

## 7. The Garden Defense Network
**Purpose:** Protection of the cultivated beds — *from everyone,* including the rabbits who
claim them. A neutral guild of the garden's smaller residents.
**Membership:** The Bee Patrol (armored deterrent), Sir Pinch and the beetles (slow infantry),
the hovering insects of the Tomato Cathedral.
**History:** An uneasy mutual-defense pact among creatures who agree on nothing except that the
garden should not be trampled. The Bee Patrol is its teeth; everyone respects the Bee Patrol,
Sakura most of all (she learned once).
**Standing with Sakura:** **Wary Peace.** She gives the bees a respectful berth. They let her
pass.
**Goals:** Defend the beds. Be left alone. Sting only as a last resort.

## 8. The Ant Nation
**Purpose:** The continuation of the Ant Nation.
**Membership:** Uncountable. The realm's largest and oldest civilization by far.
**History:** Has built an entire society beneath everyone's notice. Moves monuments of crumb.
Has never acknowledged the war, the Warden, the factions, or the vorg. Will, in all
likelihood, outlast the entire mythology.
**Standing with Sakura:** **Truce (by mutual obliviousness).** They do not know she exists.
She has decided not to escalate.
**Goals:** Unknowable, vast, patient. The Ant Nation plays a longer game than anyone.

## 9. The Firefly Court
**Purpose:** Unknown. Convenes only at summer dusk, only near the Unknown Regions.
**Membership:** The fireflies. Seasonal. Possibly ceremonial. Possibly a signal.
**History:** The one organization associated with the far corner that brings Sakura *peace*
rather than wariness. She watches the Court. She does not interfere. What they are doing out
there at the edge of the mystery, no one knows.
**Standing with Sakura:** **Truce**, and something gentler than truce. Possibly the realm's
only *sacred* relationship.
**Goals:** Unknown. See `vorg-mythology.md`. Do not resolve.

## 10. The Diplomatic Border (The Cat's Court)
**Purpose:** The sovereign domain of the Neighbor's Cat, bordering the realm along the
Diplomatic Fence.
**Membership:** One. Lord Whiskers (allegedly). A court of a single, supremely composed
monarch.
**History:** The realm's only foreign *land* border, held in a cold peace neither party will
formalize or break. Site of an unresolved diplomatic incident. Neither has crossed. Neither
has stood down.
**Standing with Sakura:** **Cold War**, between possible equals. She may respect the Cat more
than any creature alive. She would die before admitting it.
**Goals:** Maintain the border. Win by composure. Never blink first.

## 11. The Order of the Dusk Vigil
**Purpose:** To hold the line at the edge of the known world. Membership: one.
**Membership:** Sakura. The only member. The only one who shows up. Every night.
**History:** Not an organization others joined — an order Sakura founded alone, by simply
appearing at the frontier each dusk and refusing to advance or retreat. The realm's most
solemn institution, conducted by a single dog, witnessed by one believing child.
**Standing with Sakura:** **She is the Order.**
**Goals:** Hold the line. Whatever that means. Until whatever it is for.

## Organization Law

- No faction is evil. Each is sincere, chartered, and a little absurd.
- Standing with Sakura can shift through incidents — a truce can fray, a rivalry can soften
  (see Old Greycoat). Track changes in `incident-archive.md`.
- New factions should fill an empty niche (a new territory, a new species, a foreign power),
  not duplicate an existing one.
- The Patio Authority's denials of the vorg must remain firm and suspicious. Never let the
  Authority be right.
- A child should grasp the rivalries instantly. An adult should recognize the bureaucracy.
  Both should enjoy that a backyard has a foreign policy.
