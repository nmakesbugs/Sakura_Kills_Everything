# Creature Catalog — The Recurring Cast of the Backyard

> Tier 1 canon. The backyard is not populated by *animals*. It is populated by
> *characters* — named, recurring, each with a history and a grudge. This catalog turns
> generic fauna into a living ensemble. For threat classification doctrine, see
> `creature-taxonomy.md`; this is the who's-who.
>
> Every creature here is a future quest, rival, ally, or mystery. Some are friends. Most
> are not. None are evil — this is a comedy. They are simply *committed to their side.*

**Legend:** 🐿️ Squirrel · 🐇 Rabbit · 🐦 Bird · 🐿 Chipmunk · 🐛 Insect · ❓ Unclassified
**Alignment:** RIVAL · ALLY · NEUTRAL · MYSTERY

---

## THE SQUIRRELS — *The Eternal Adversary*
*Tier S. Years of pursuit. Zero confirmed catches. The squirrels know this. It informs
their entire demeanor.*

### 1. The Squirrel 🐿️ — MYSTERY/RIVAL
**The singular, eternal nemesis.** Not *a* squirrel — *the* Squirrel, the shapeshifting
archetype Sakura has chased her whole career and never caught. Every chase, in her mind,
is against this one creature. It is fast, it is smug, and it is, in the deepest canon
sense, immortal, because it is really the idea of the chase itself.
**Relationship with Sakura:** The defining relationship of her life. She does not want to
end it. She wants it to continue forever, which — accidentally — is exactly what happens.
**Future potential:** The final "boss" that can never truly be caught. The heart of every
mode.

### 2. Acorn 🐿️ — RIVAL
The brash young field-crosser. Reckless, fast, and convinced he is faster than he is. Takes
the Open Field at a dead sprint when the smarter move is the fence. Has come closer to
being caught than any squirrel in history — twice — and treats both near-deaths as great
victories.
**History:** The "almost" of *The Securing of the Open Field* most often refers to Acorn.
**Future potential:** The rival you *almost* get. Rookie energy. Crowd favorite.

### 3. Field Marshal Bramble 🐿️ — RIVAL
The presumed squirrel high command. Allegedly directs operations from the upper branches of
the Old Tree. Never seen at ground level. Never hurried. Believed to assign the sentries and
plan the fence-line logistics. May be a myth. The family is not sure he exists. Soren is
certain he does not. Cias is certain he does.
**Relationship with Sakura:** Cold war. She has never reached him. She circles his fortress
daily. He has, reportedly, never once looked down.
**Future potential:** The unseen mastermind. The campaign antagonist who is mostly rumor.

### 4. The Corner Sentry 🐿️ — RIVAL
The lookout said to be permanently posted at the Corner Post. Whether it is always the same
squirrel or a rotating watch is unknown. Issues the alarm chitter that empties the field
the instant Sakura commits. Sakura considers the Sentry her most personal enemy: not a
fighter, but a *spoiler.*
**Future potential:** Stealth mechanics. Beat the Sentry, get a clean run at the field.

### 5. Two-Tail 🐿️ — RIVAL/MYSTERY
A squirrel reported to have an unusually large or doubled tail. Possibly two squirrels
mistaken for one. Possibly one remarkable squirrel. Sightings are inconsistent and heated.
A minor cryptid *within* the squirrel population.
**Future potential:** A collectible-sighting mystery. Is it real? Log your evidence.

### 6. Nibbles the Bold 🐿️ — RIVAL
The squirrel who comes onto the Great Patio itself — into the seat of Sakura's power — to
steal from the very heart of the realm. An act of breathtaking nerve. Sakura takes each
incursion as a personal constitutional crisis.
**Future potential:** Home-defense events. The enemy at the gates.

### 7. Old Greycoat 🐿️ — NEUTRAL
The ancient squirrel. Slow now, grey at the muzzle, no longer running the field. Watches
from a low branch. It is whispered that Old Greycoat and Sakura have an understanding — two
veterans of a long war, past the need to chase. She does not pursue him. No one knows why.
**Future potential:** A poignant, optional, non-combat character. The truce no one mentions.

## THE RABBITS — *The Suspicious Ones*
*Tier A. All rabbits are presumed suspicious. The presumption is the policy.*

### 8. General Thistle 🐇 — RIVAL
The rabbit warlord of the Garden Frontier. Commands from Warlord's Hollow beneath the
largest bed. Master of stillness — can hold so motionless he ceases, tactically, to exist —
followed by explosive, contemptuous escape. Regards the garden as sovereign rabbit
territory and Sakura as a recurring border dispute.
**Relationship with Sakura:** Mutual, dignified hostility. They have stared into each
other's eyes across the mulch many times. Nothing has ever come of it. This is, somehow,
the most intense rivalry in the realm.
**Future potential:** The Garden campaign's central antagonist. A war of patience.

### 9. The Twitch 🐇 — RIVAL
General Thistle's nervous lieutenant. Gives away ambushes by twitching at the wrong moment.
Means well (for a rabbit). Survives mostly by being near calmer rabbits. Beloved by the
family precisely because he is bad at being suspicious.
**Future potential:** Comic-relief rival. The enemy who keeps blowing his own cover.

### 10. Duchess Clover 🐇 — RIVAL/NEUTRAL
The elegant rabbit of the open lawn at dawn. Aristocratic, unbothered, grazes in full view
as if daring anyone to comment. Believed to outrank Thistle socially while outranking no one
militarily. A diplomat, not a soldier.
**Future potential:** Diplomacy mechanics — can rabbits be negotiated with? She is the
one to ask.

### 11. The Kits 🐇 — NEUTRAL
The young rabbits. Small, round, catastrophically cute. Sakura's doctrine *insists* they
are suspicious; her actual behavior around them is confused and gentle. A documented crack
in the Warden's hard line.
**Future potential:** The "she can't even pretend to hunt these" beat. Pure wholesome.

### 12. The Phantom of the Garden 🐇 — MYSTERY
The rabbit no one has clearly seen — known only by signs: a disturbed bed, a flattened
hollow of grass still warm, prints leading nowhere. Possibly Thistle. Possibly an unknown
power. The garden's resident ghost story.
**Future potential:** Investigation gameplay. Track the phantom by clues alone.

## THE BIRDS — *Under Surveillance*
*Tier A. One confirmed catch in all of history. The birds have not forgotten. Neither has Sakura.*

### 13. The Bird (Deceased) 🐦 — HISTORICAL
The one confirmed catch. Taken from the air in **The Bird Incident** — the single greatest
achievement of Sakura's career. Spoken of in reverent, slightly stunned tones. Not a
character so much as a turning point. (See `sakura-canon-bible.md`.)
**Future potential:** The legend referenced across all modes. The proof it can be done.

### 14. Captain Flit 🐦 — RIVAL
The cocky robin of the Great Patio. Lands *just* out of reach, again and again, with the
timing of a creature who has run the numbers. The presumed leader of patio airspace
violations. Believes — correctly — that he is the safest creature in the yard.
**Relationship with Sakura:** Her most *aggravating* rival. Not dangerous. Just smug, and
airborne, and right there.
**Future potential:** Duck Hunt's signature recurring rival. The one you want most.

### 15. The Crow Council Envoy 🐦 — MYSTERY/RIVAL
A large crow, glossy and unhurried, that visits from the wider neighborhood. Watches.
Does not flee. Reports, the family suspects, to powers beyond the fence. Treats the yard as
foreign territory it is merely surveying.
**History:** Connected to the rumored **Crow Council** (see `organizations.md`). Tactical
assessment: pending, possibly forever.
**Future potential:** The outside world made visible. A questline that leaves the yard.

### 16. The Sparrow Squadron 🐦 — RIVAL
A fast-moving flock that arrives as one and scatters as many. Impossible to track
individually. Treats the feeder and the patio as a contested airfield. Numbers are their
doctrine.
**Future potential:** Wave-based mode content. The swarm.

### 17. Mourning Pair (Coo & Roo) 🐦 — NEUTRAL
Two doves of unshakable calm. Sit on the fence and *coo* through the most dramatic events,
utterly unbothered. Sakura has decided they are not a threat, possibly out of respect for
how little they care.
**Future potential:** Ambient peace. The creatures that make the yard feel like home.

### 18. The Hawk (Rumored) 🐦 — MYSTERY
A shadow that crosses the field on rare afternoons; a hush that falls over every other
creature at once. Never confirmed at close range. The one airborne power Sakura watches
*without* barking — which means something.
**Future potential:** A rare, weather-tied apex event. The sky's own vorg.

## THE CHIPMUNKS — *The Underground*
*Smaller, faster, lower. The squirrels' subterranean cousins and rivals.*

### 19. Pockets 🐿 — RIVAL
The chipmunk who vanishes into impossible gaps — stone walls, root systems, the spaces
between things. Cheeks perpetually full of contraband. Operates the realm's informal
smuggling routes beneath the surface.
**Future potential:** Whack-a-mole / pop-up mechanics. The enemy who uses the *third*
dimension — down.

### 20. The Tunnel Authority 🐿 — MYSTERY/RIVAL
A presumed network of chipmunks said to maintain passages beneath the entire yard. If real,
they move under Sakura's patrols completely undetected. Evidence: holes that appear
overnight. The family files this under "concerning."
**Future potential:** A hidden underground map layer. The war below the war.

### 21. Stripe 🐿 — NEUTRAL
A single bold chipmunk who freezes in the open, statue-still, betting everything on not being
seen. Sakura has walked past Stripe many times. The bet keeps working. Neither will admit
the other won.
**Future potential:** Stealth comedy. The creature hiding in plain sight, badly, successfully.

## THE INSECTS — *The Small Theater*
*Tier C. Beneath her life's work, but acceptable when slow-moving. A whole comic world at
ground level.*

### 22. The Buzz 🐛 — NEUTRAL/RIVAL
The single fly that gets into the proceedings and turns a dignified Warden into a snapping,
spinning, undignified blur. The great humbler. Has never been caught and never been
respected and somehow always wins.
**Future potential:** Comic interrupt event. Chaos Mode's smallest agent.

### 23. The Bee Patrol 🐛 — NEUTRAL
Industrious, armored, and — Sakura has learned, once, the hard way — not to be snapped at.
The one insect she gives a respectful berth. A lesson the catalog records so others need not
repeat it.
**Future potential:** The "do not touch" creature. Risk/reward restraint.

### 24. Sir Pinch (The Beetle) 🐛 — NEUTRAL
A large, armored, slow-marching beetle of immense dignity. Crosses the patio like it owns
the deed. Sakura escorts rather than attacks — uncertain whether this is prey, peer, or
small furniture.
**Future potential:** A recurring "what even is this" gag character.

### 25. The Ant Nation 🐛 — MYSTERY
The most numerous and organized civilization in the realm, beneath everyone's notice. Moves
crumbs the size of monuments. Unbothered by the entire war raging above. May outlast all of
it.
**Future potential:** A scale-shift mode. The tiny epic. The civilization that doesn't know
Sakura exists.

### 26. The Firefly Court 🐛 — ALLY/MYSTERY
Summer-only. Appears at dusk near the Unknown Regions. Sakura does not chase them — she
*watches.* The only creatures associated with the far corner that bring her peace rather
than wariness. A rare gentle light at the edge of the mystery.
**Future potential:** Seasonal wonder event. A possible (friendly?) thread to the vorg.

## THE OUTSIDERS & MYSTERIES — *Beyond the Doctrine*

### 27. The Neighbor's Cat (Lord Whiskers, allegedly) ❓ — RIVAL/NEUTRAL
The realm's only foreign head of state. Sits upon the Diplomatic Fence with insufferable
composure. Classification disputed: predator, peer, or provocateur? Neither side has
crossed the border. Neither has stood down. A cold peace held by mutual, dignified refusal
to acknowledge the other exists.
**Relationship with Sakura:** The great stalemate. Possibly the only creature she considers
an *equal.* She would never say so.
**Future potential:** Recurring rival-or-ally. A diplomacy/standoff system all its own.

### 28. The Toad of the Drainpipe ❓ — MYSTERY
A toad reported near the Whispering Gutter after rain. Appears, regards the proceedings with
ancient indifference, and is gone by morning. Says nothing. Means, possibly, everything.
**Future potential:** Rain-tied oracle character. Cryptic, optional, beloved.

### 29. The Mole (The Unseen Excavator) ❓ — MYSTERY
Never seen. Known only by the slow rise of fresh earth across the lawn. Reshapes the very
ground of the realm from below, indifferent to all factions. Sakura finds the mounds
deeply, personally offensive.
**Future potential:** Terrain that changes overnight. An enemy you fight by its works alone.

### 30. The Vorg ❓ — MYSTERY
Not catalogued. Cannot be catalogued. Existence unconfirmed. Listed here only so that its
absence is on the record. Something is at the far corner at dusk. Sakura holds the line. The
catalog ends, appropriately, at the edge of what it cannot name.
**Future potential:** Everything. See `vorg-mythology.md`. Do not resolve.

## Catalog Law

- Creatures are **characters, not stats.** Each should be recognizable by behavior alone.
- No creature is evil. Each is loyal to its own side of a fundamentally silly war.
- Allies, rivals, and mysteries should stay roughly balanced as the catalog grows.
- A new creature earns a name the moment it earns an *incident.* Anonymous fauna become
  named cast through the things they do. See `incident-archive.md`.
- The roster is open. The realm can hold many more. Aim, over time, for a backyard as
  crowded with personalities as a small and very dramatic nation.
