## Identity

**Full name:** Sakura  
**Species:** Dog  
**Classification:** Apex backyard predator  
**Jurisdiction:** The Backyard and all adjacent territories  
**Status:** Active. Extremely active.

---

## Confirmed Behaviors

- Sakura hunts with complete sincerity. She does not know she is funny.
- Sakura has never successfully caught a squirrel. This does not discourage her.
- Sakura has caught at least one bird. This is mentioned with pride.
- Sakura patrols the fence line on a self-determined schedule.
- Sakura monitors the yard from interior windows when denied outdoor access.
- Sakura responds to squirrel appearances with immediate and total commitment.
- Sakura has a signature alert bark. Family members can identify the specific target by bark pattern.
- Sakura is not afraid of larger animals. This is considered unwise by outside observers.

## Canonical Incidents

*Real family stories. Each incident is dated (or marked as undated legend), named, and
described. These incidents become game events. Nothing here is invented — all are
verified by family consensus.*

> The Bird Incident below is the **founding incident**, kept here in the Bible for primacy.
> The full history of the realm — 27 incidents and growing, each with its official legend and
> its likely reality — lives in **`incident-archive.md`**.

### The Bird Incident (INC-001)
Date: Approximate — undated, a foundational family legend
Location: The Backyard
Witness: Tanisha (discovered the aftermath)
Creatures involved: One bird (deceased)
Outcome: Tanisha stepped outside and noticed Sakura had blood on her nose. Investigating,
she found the bird — already dead. Sakura had caught it clean out of the air, thrashed it
thoroughly, and then rolled in it. She was extremely pleased with herself and saw no
reason not to be.
Canonical truth: One confirmed aerial catch. Her single greatest achievement. Birds must die.

> **Game note:** This incident is the canonical basis for Duck Hunt's *bird* target —
> the only quarry Sakura has ever confirmed catching. (Squirrels remain at zero.)
