# Expansion Seeds — Story Hooks for Years of Content

> Tier 1 canon (as raw material). Sixty-plus story seeds — not gameplay specs, *stories.* Each
> is a spark that could grow into a quest, an incident, a season, a rival, or a mystery. They
> exist so the realm never runs dry. Pull one off the shelf whenever the world needs a new
> thread.
>
> **Rule:** Seeds are possibilities, not promises. Any seed touching the vorg must deepen the
> mystery, never resolve it. Any seed reaching real family events outranks the invented ones.

---

## How to Use a Seed

1. Pick a seed that fits the season, mode, or mood you need.
2. Grow it: who's involved (cast/catalog), where (map), which faction (organizations).
3. Play it out; if it lands, **log it as a numbered incident** in `incident-archive.md`.
4. A used seed becomes history. Cross it off; the realm has grown.

## 🐿️ The Squirrel Front (Seeds 1–10)
1. **A new rival appears** — a squirrel faster than Acorn arrives on the fence line. The field
   has a new fastest runner. Acorn is not handling it well.
2. **The Sentry goes silent** — for three days no alarm chitter sounds. Is the Corner Sentry
   gone? Captured? On strike? The field is eerily open.
3. **Field Marshal Bramble is sighted at ground level** — for the first time, the unseen
   high command allegedly descends. Or someone *dressed* as him did.
4. **The acorn cache is discovered** — Sakura unearths a squirrel's buried hoard. Does she
   guard it, return it, or roll in it?
5. **A squirrel civil war** — the Directorate splits into the fence faction and the tree
   faction. Sakura is invited (she thinks) to mediate.
6. **The truce with Old Greycoat is tested** — a young squirrel mocks the old veteran. Will
   Sakura honor the unspoken peace or defend her former rival?
7. **Nibbles gets greedy** — the patio raider attempts the indoors. The Threshold is breached.
8. **The Great Tree Census** — the family attempts to count the squirrels in the Old Tree.
   The number keeps changing. It always keeps changing.
9. **A squirrel leaves a gift** — a single acorn placed deliberately at the Root Throne. Peace
   offering? Taunt? The realm debates for a week.
10. **The fastest chase ever recorded** — conditions align for the closest non-bird pursuit in
    history. Will it beat the Acorn Near-Catch?

## 🐇 The Rabbit Question (Seeds 11–18)
11. **General Thistle issues a demand** — formal (rabbit) notice that the garden is sovereign.
    The Patio Authority must respond.
12. **A rabbit defects** — the Twitch, tired of being bad at suspicion, seeks asylum on the
    patio. Diplomacy ensues.
13. **The Phantom is finally glimpsed** — for one second. No one agrees on what they saw.
14. **Duchess Clover requests a summit** — the realm's first formal dog-rabbit diplomatic talks.
    Held across the mulch. Nothing is agreed. Everyone is very dignified.
15. **The Kits grow up** — last spring's babies return as full suspects. Sakura's hard line is
    tested by nostalgia.
16. **A garden mystery** — vegetables vanish faster than the rabbits could manage. A third
    power? (The chipmunks? Something else?)
17. **Warlord's Hollow is found empty** — the Coalition has relocated overnight. Where? Why?
18. **The staring record falls** — Sakura and Thistle hold the longest motionless standoff
    ever timed. Soren is deeply unsettled.

## 🐦 The Skies (Seeds 19–26)
19. **A bird summit is announced** — the Bird Council convenes a great assembly. Massed birds,
    one afternoon, everywhere.
20. **Captain Flit nests** — the cocky robin settles in and becomes, briefly, *grounded* and
    vulnerable. A second Bird Incident has never been more possible.
21. **The Hawk returns, and lands** — the rumored apex actually perches in the realm. The Hush
    becomes a confrontation.
22. **The Crow Council sends a message** — the Envoy leaves something behind: a shiny object, a
    feather, a sign. Translation attempts begin.
23. **The migration is early** — the skies empty weeks ahead of schedule. Something spooked
    them. (The vorg? The Hawk? The weather?)
24. **A lost fledgling** — a young bird, grounded and helpless, on the patio. Sakura's doctrine
    versus Sakura's heart. (Wholesome.)
25. **The feeder is rationed** — winter scarcity forces the birds and the bold squirrel into an
    uneasy alliance at the feeder. Sakura watches an enemy coalition form.
26. **The dove speaks** — the Mourning Pair, who only ever *coo,* do something out of
    character. The calmest creatures in the realm break their calm. Why?

## 🐿 The Underground (Seeds 27–31)
27. **Strange new holes appear** — overnight, across the lawn. The Tunnel Authority is
    expanding. Sakura declares war on geology.
28. **Pockets is caught above ground** — the smuggler, exposed in daylight, cheeks full. A rare
    surface engagement.
29. **The tunnels collapse a patrol route** — the ground itself betrays the Warden. The route
    must be re-drawn for the first time since the Founding.
30. **A map is found** — the family discovers the holes form a *pattern.* The Underground has a
    plan. (Or it's random. It's probably random.)
31. **Stripe is finally noticed** — Sakura, after years, *sees* the statue-still chipmunk. Now
    what?

## 🐛 The Small Theater (Seeds 32–36)
32. **A bee swarm passes through** — the Garden Defense Network mobilizes. Sakura gives the
    widest berth of her career.
33. **Sir Pinch's long march** — the dignified beetle attempts to cross the entire realm.
    Sakura appoints herself escort.
34. **The Buzz wins** — a single fly humbles the Warden so thoroughly it becomes a logged
    incident. Chaos Mode's smallest legend.
35. **The Ant Nation moves a monument** — the ants relocate something improbably large. The
    realm notices the ants for the first time, briefly, with awe.
36. **The first firefly of summer** — a single early light at the corner. The Court is coming.
    Cias is beside themselves.

## ❓ The Frontier & The Vorg (Seeds 37–44) — *deepen, never resolve*
37. **New tracks at the Far Corner** — clearer than before. Still unidentifiable. The photo is,
    again, blurry. (One stone added.)
38. **The Dusk Vigil runs long** — Sakura holds the line past dark for the first time. The
    family waits at the open door.
39. **The Cold Spot moves** — the inexplicably cool patch is reported in a *new* location.
    Soren has no explanation and refuses to admit it.
40. **The Firefly Court lights the corner** — for one summer night, something at the frontier
    is *almost* visible. Almost.
41. **Sketch D is submitted** — a new vorg sketch contradicts all prior ones *and* Sketch C
    (the blank page). The archive accepts both.
42. **Old Greycoat looks at the corner** — the old squirrel, who may know what the vorg is,
    turns toward the Unknown Regions. Sakura watches him watch it.
43. **A sound from the gutter** — the Whispering Gutter makes a noise that is, this time, not
    water. Probably. The Toad of the Drainpipe is consulted (says nothing).
44. **The Long Watch** — the coldest, longest night. The annual single piece of vorg evidence
    appears in the snow at dawn, then melts. (One stone, then gone.)

## 🤝 Diplomacy & Factions (Seeds 45–50)
45. **The Cat crosses the border** — for the first time, the Neighbor's Cat sets one paw in the
    realm. The cold peace is over. Or just beginning.
46. **The Patio Authority holds an election** — bureaucratic comedy. Sakura runs unopposed,
    again, because no one is brave enough.
47. **A faction sues for peace** — one organization formally seeks alliance with Sakura. Which?
    And what do they want?
48. **The Crow Council issues a denial** — a foreign power formally denies involvement in
    something no one accused them of. Suspicious.
49. **The Bee Patrol and the Tunnel Authority clash** — a turf war erupts *beneath the garden*
    between factions that don't usually meet. Sakura is, gloriously, irrelevant to it.
50. **A summit of all factions** — the realm's first great assembly. Squirrels, rabbits, birds,
    and the Cat, in one place, under truce. What could possibly go wrong. (Nolyn is present.)

## 💛 Wholesome & Domestic (Seeds 51–56)
51. **Another forgotten toy** — a second lost artifact surfaces. Pure joy, no enemy, fully
    canon.
52. **First snow, again** — the annual delighted disbelief. It never gets old, for her or for us.
53. **A birthday in the realm** — the anniversary of the Founding (the day Sakura arrived). The
    realm celebrates its Warden.
54. **The good chair is claimed** — a quiet domestic territorial dispute, settled with a sigh
    and a shared cushion.
55. **A guest dog visits** — a friend comes to the realm. Sakura must decide: rival, ally, or
    co-Warden? (Spoiler: instant best friend.)
56. **The nap that defended the realm** — Sakura sleeps through an entire incident and the yard
    is fine. The Found Victory in its purest form.

## 🌍 The Wider World (Seeds 57–62)
57. **A sound from two streets over** — the deer, rumored, returns to legend. Response protocol
    still unknown.
58. **The Large Crow is confirmed** — the neighborhood crow finally takes a clear place in the
    catalog. The outside world steps closer.
59. **A storm reshapes the realm** — weather rearranges the geography overnight. A new
    territory is born; an old one is lost. The map must be redrawn.
60. **The fence is repaired** — a real-world fence fix closes the Knothole Gate. The squirrels
    adapt within hours. The realm marvels at their resilience.
61. **A new family member / new audience** — the realm gains a witness. The canon grows a voice.
    (Tied to real life; outranks invention.)
62. **The archive is read aloud** — the family reads the incident archive together and, in
    doing so, makes all of it true at once. The mythology becomes a tradition.

## Seed Law
- Seeds are sparks, not scripts. Grow them to fit the moment.
- Vorg seeds deepen the mystery; they never explain it. One stone at a time.
- Real family events always outrank invented seeds — when life writes a story, plant it here.
- A used seed becomes a logged incident. The shelf is meant to be drawn down and refilled
  forever.
- There is no last seed. A backyard civilization generates stories indefinitely. Keep planting.
