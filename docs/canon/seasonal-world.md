# The Seasonal World

> Tier 1 canon. The backyard is not a fixed stage. It turns with the year — and so does the
> war. Each season rewrites the creature behavior, the threats, the opportunities, and the
> legends. The realm Sakura defends in July is not the realm she defends in January, and she
> defends both with identical, total commitment.
>
> This document is the engine of **seasonal events, limited-time content, and the yearly
> rhythm** that keeps the game alive across the calendar. Four seasons. One Warden. Endless
> recurrence.

---

## The Turning of the Year (Overview)

```
        SPRING  →  SUMMER  →  FALL  →  WINTER  →  (SPRING)
        The      The       The      The
        Awakening Reign     Reckoning Long Watch
        ─────────────────────────────────────────────
        new life  full war  the      the
                            harvest  quiet
```

Each season has a **mood**, a shift in every faction's behavior, signature **events**, new
**threats** and **opportunities**, a **legend** that only makes sense in that season, and
clear **gameplay hooks.** Sakura's doctrine never changes. Everything around it does.

---

## 🌱 SPRING — The Awakening

**Mood:** Hope, mud, and the return of the enemy. The realm wakes up and immediately resumes
hostilities.

### Creature Behavior
- **Squirrels:** Frantic and everywhere — rebuilding, foraging, racing. The fence-line
  highway reopens at full volume. Acorn returns from wherever squirrels winter, faster and
  cockier.
- **Rabbits:** **The Kits arrive.** The Garden Frontier fills with small round suspects, and
  Sakura's hard line visibly cracks (INC-015). The Rabbit Coalition is at its largest and its
  most adorable, which is tactically devastating to her.
- **Birds:** Nesting season. The Bird Council is busy, territorial, and *loud.* Captain Flit
  is at his most provocative, defending a nest somewhere just out of reach.
- **Insects:** First stirrings. The Buzz returns. The Bee Patrol re-musters.

### Events
- **The Thaw Patrol** — The first patrol of the new year. The route survived the winter. She
  is overjoyed and surprised, as always.
- **The Mud Season** — The Bald Spot becomes the Mud Spot. Sakura tracks the realm indoors,
  one paw at a time. The Patio Authority is displeased.
- **The Return of Acorn** — A signature rival re-enters the field.

### Threats & Opportunities
- **Threat:** Maximum squirrel activity; the enemy is rebuilding faster than she can patrol.
- **Opportunity:** Nesting birds are grounded more often — the *theoretical* best window for a
  second Bird Incident. (It will not happen. But the odds, this season, are at their lifetime
  peak.)

### Legend of the Season
**The Greening of the Old Tree.** When the Sentinel buds, the squirrel high command is said
to convene for the year's first council. The Siege resumes in earnest.

### Gameplay Hooks
- Spring-only Kits encounters (wholesome, non-combat).
- "Best odds of the year" bird mode modifier.
- Mud mechanics; route-resurrection event.

## ☀️ SUMMER — The Reign

**Mood:** Heat, abundance, full war, and the long golden afternoons that are the soul of the
whole mythology.

### Creature Behavior
- **Squirrels:** At full strength and full confidence. The Directorate operates openly. Patio
  raids (Nibbles) peak. This is the height of the conflict.
- **Rabbits:** The garden is overgrown and fragrant; the Coalition holds it easily. General
  Thistle is at the peak of his power, hidden in deep cover.
- **Birds:** The Sparrow Squadron contests the feeder daily. The rumored Hawk crosses on the
  hottest, stillest afternoons (INC-017, The Hush).
- **Insects:** Peak season. The Tomato Cathedral hums. Sir Pinch marches. And at dusk — **the
  Firefly Court convenes** near the Unknown Regions.

### Events
- **The Sun Patch Campaign** — Sakura tracks the migrating warmth across the field with more
  devotion than she tracks any enemy. A campaign of pure leisure.
- **The Garden at Full Distraction** — Tactical focus is at its yearly low. Pursuits dissolve
  into sniffing constantly (INC-013).
- **The Firefly Vigil** — Summer-only. The Court appears; Sakura watches in rare peace; the
  vorg mystery glows at the edge of the dark.

### Threats & Opportunities
- **Threat:** The Hush — a rare apex-aerial event. The whole yard goes silent.
- **Opportunity:** Long days = the most patrol hours of the year. The realm has never been
  more thoroughly, pointlessly secured.

### Legend of the Season
**The Night the Court Lit the Corner.** It is whispered that one summer the Firefly Court
shone so bright at the Unknown Regions that, for a moment, *something at the corner was almost
visible.* Almost. (See `vorg-mythology.md`. Never resolve.)

### Gameplay Hooks
- Firefly Court seasonal wonder event (vorg-adjacent, gentle).
- The Hush as a rare, weather-tied apex encounter.
- Sun Patch "rest" mini-loop; peak Chaos Mode (Nolyn's season).

## 🍂 FALL — The Reckoning

**Mood:** Urgency, gold and rust, and a war that intensifies as the year runs out.

### Creature Behavior
- **Squirrels:** **Peak menace.** The great hoarding. The Directorate works frantically,
  burying treasure across the realm — and the Drop Zone beneath the Old Tree becomes a
  bombardment of falling acorns (provocations? gifts? both). The most squirrel-saturated
  season of the year.
- **Rabbits:** Withdrawing toward Warlord's Hollow as the garden dies back. Cover thins; the
  Coalition is, briefly, *exposed.* General Thistle is more catchable in fall than at any
  other time — which means not at all, but closer.
- **Birds:** Gathering to leave. The Bird Council holds its great migratory summits. Whole
  formations cross the realm. The Crow Council Envoy visits more often.
- **Insects:** Fading. The last of the Buzz. The Firefly Court goes dark for the year.

### Events
- **The Acorn Bombardment** — The Drop Zone activates. Sakura under fire from above, taking
  it personally.
- **The Leaf Insurgency** — Every falling leaf is a moving target. Sakura engages thousands.
  The realm's premier "Suspicious Leaf Investigator" content. She investigates each one with
  total sincerity.
- **The Great Migration Summit** — Massed birds, briefly, everywhere. A spectacle and a
  challenge.

### Threats & Opportunities
- **Threat:** Sensory overload — falling leaves, hoarding squirrels, departing flocks. The
  realm is chaos.
- **Opportunity:** Thinning cover exposes the rabbits; the hoarding squirrels are distracted
  and ground-bound. The hunting is, on paper, never better.

### Legend of the Season
**The Day the Sky Emptied.** When the last migratory flock departs, the realm holds a quiet
that even Sakura respects. The skies belong, for a season, to no one.

### Gameplay Hooks
- Leaf-target events (achievement: "Suspicious Leaf Investigator").
- Acorn Bombardment defense mode.
- Migration-summit wave content; rabbit "exposed cover" window.

## ❄️ WINTER — The Long Watch

**Mood:** Stillness, cold, scarcity, and the strange intimacy of a realm reduced to its bones.

### Creature Behavior
- **Squirrels:** Scarce. Most retreat to the Old Tree; only the hardiest cross the field, and
  they cross *fast.* Every sighting is an event. The war goes quiet but never ends.
- **Rabbits:** Largely vanished. The Garden Frontier sleeps. A single set of prints in fresh
  snow becomes a major incident (the Phantom's clearest evidence appears in winter).
- **Birds:** The winter residents only — the Mourning Pair endure, and the feeder becomes the
  most important site in the realm, a contested oasis.
- **Insects:** Gone. The small theater is dark until spring.

### Events
- **The First Snow Patrol** — Sakura experiences snow with the same delighted disbelief every
  single year. The realm transformed. Tracks everywhere. Joy.
- **The Feeder Siege** — With food scarce, the feeder zone becomes the season's central
  conflict. Birds, the bold squirrel, and Sakura's surveillance all converge.
- **The Tracks in the Snow** — Winter is the great season of *evidence.* Every creature leaves
  a record. The Phantom, the chipmunks, and — possibly — *the vorg* leave clearer signs than
  at any other time (a deliberate, dangerous gift to the mystery; one piece per winter, never
  a conclusion).

### Threats & Opportunities
- **Threat:** Scarcity sharpens every conflict. The cold itself is an adversary (the realm's
  apex environmental threat after the vacuum).
- **Opportunity:** Snow records everything. The best season to *read the realm* — for
  investigation gameplay, winter is supreme.

### Legend of the Season
**The Long Watch.** It is said that on the coldest, stillest night, the Dusk Vigil lasts until
dawn — that Sakura, alone, holds the frontier through the entire dark of the year's longest
night, and that whatever is at the corner holds with her, on the other side, doing the same.
Neither crosses. Neither leaves. (Unconfirmed. Sacred. Never resolved.)

### Gameplay Hooks
- Snow-tracking investigation mode (winter's signature system).
- Feeder Siege defense event.
- The annual single piece of vorg evidence (frontier content).
- First Snow joy event (pure wholesome, like the Forgotten Toy).

## Cross-Season Systems

### The Year as a Loop
The game's calendar should *turn,* visibly. Returning players should find the realm changed
with the real-world season. The squirrels they fought in spring are hoarding by fall and
gone by winter. The continuity is the reward.

### Seasonal Reputation
Faction standing can shift seasonally — the Rabbit Coalition is mighty in summer and exposed
in fall; the Bird Council assembles in spring and departs in fall. Diplomacy has a season.

### The Vorg Across the Year
- **Spring:** quiet. The corner is just mud.
- **Summer:** the Firefly Court; the mystery glows.
- **Fall:** the corner goes dark and cold early.
- **Winter:** the clearest evidence of the year appears in the snow — one piece, then gone.

Each year adds exactly **one** new stone to the vorg mystery, tied to a season. Never a
conclusion. The mystery deepens with the calendar, forever.

---

## Seasonal Law

- Every season changes *behavior,* not just decoration. The factions must actually move
  differently.
- Each season needs at least one **wholesome** event (First Snow, the Forgotten Toy, the Kits)
  alongside its conflicts. The warmth is not optional.
- Seasonal content recurs but should *accrete* — each year, add a new wrinkle, a new legend, a
  new piece of the mystery.
- The vorg gets one seasonal stone per year. No more. No resolution.
- A child should feel the year turn. An adult should feel the years *stack.*
