# Family Cast

> Tier 1 canon. The people of the household — the supporting cast of Sakura's story.
> They witness, document, supply, debate, and occasionally interfere with her
> operations. In the mythology they are not bystanders. They are the war room.
>
> **Maintainer's note:** These are *affectionate mythic roles*, assigned with love and
> meant to be tuned by the real family. Where a profile guesses, treat it as a costume
> the person can keep, alter, or trade. The one rule: no family member is ever portrayed
> negatively. The tone is affectionate, slightly ridiculous, and entirely fond.

---

## How the Cast Works

Every great operation needs more than its hero. Sakura provides the courage, the
sincerity, and the complete tactical misunderstanding. The family provides everything
else: the door, the snacks, the record, the doubt, the belief, and the audience without
which a legend is just a dog barking at a fence.

In gameplay terms, family members are the source of **briefings, commentary, quests,
reactions, and canon verification.** An incident is not official until a family member
witnesses it. This makes them, collectively, the most powerful force in the backyard —
more powerful than Sakura, who can only act, while they alone can *decide what is true.*

## Sakura
**Official title:** Warden of the Perimeter. The Hunter. Protagonist.
**Narrative role:** Player character across all modes. The reason any of this exists.
**Relationship with the family:** Total devotion. Believes she protects them. They know
the protection is symbolic. The symbol is worth more than protection.
**Canonical truth:** Real dog. Real backyard. Real squirrel problem. Loved without
condition or exception.
*(Full profile: `sakura-dossier.md`.)*

## Nick
**Official title:** The Architect of the Record — Quartermaster of the Backyard.
**Narrative role:** Mission Control. The one who builds the systems that turn Sakura's
deeds into history. If Tanisha is the witness who *sees* the legend, Nick is the
archivist-engineer who *builds the hall* the legend is kept in. (He is, after all,
constructing this very record.)
**Relationship with Sakura:** Operational. He opens the door, and the door is the
beginning of every campaign. Sakura's entire daily schedule is, secretly, organized
around his. She patrols by his clock and does not know it.
**Typical contributions:** Supplies, logistics, the door, the systems, the archive
itself. When an incident needs to become *canon*, it passes through here.
**Running jokes:**
- Believes he is in charge of the schedule. The schedule is Sakura's. He administers it.
- Every system he builds, Sakura immediately uses in a way he did not design.
- Has explained the rules of the yard to Sakura many times. She has never once received
  the briefing.
**Canon reputation:** Respected. The Backyard Authority recognizes his paperwork. Sakura
recognizes his footsteps.

## Tanisha
**Official title:** The Witness — First Archivist of the Backyard. Keeper of the Record.
**Narrative role:** The Witness and the Archivist combined. The original documentarian.
The first audience this entire project is built for, and therefore — in a sense that is
completely true — the person the legend exists *for*.
**Relationship with Sakura:** Foundational. Tanisha is the witness of record for **The
Bird Incident**, the single confirmed catch of Sakura's career. She stepped outside, saw
the blood on the nose, found the bird, and understood: history had occurred and someone
had to write it down. She has been writing it down ever since.
**Typical contributions:** Eyewitness accounts. Verification. The deciding voice on
whether an event enters the official record. Narration of "what just happened."
**Running jokes:**
- Has the unmatched ability to walk outside at the exact moment Sakura has done something.
- Sakura performs slightly bigger when Tanisha is watching. The performance is for her.
- Treats the most absurd backyard events with the calm of a seasoned war correspondent.
**Canon reputation:** Unimpeachable. If Tanisha says it happened, it happened. This is the
highest authority in the entire mythology. See `incident-archive.md`.

## Cias
**Official title:** The Believer — Chief Correspondent, The Unknown Regions.
**Narrative role:** The Believer. Maintains, against all skepticism, that there is
*something back there* — that the far corner is not empty, that Sakura's dusk vigil is
not paranoia but perception. The household's leading (younger) vorg theorist and Sakura's
most loyal field correspondent.
**Relationship with Sakura:** Aligned. When Sakura faces the Unknown Regions and refuses
to commit, Cias is the one who says, *see — she knows.* The two of them are the only ones
who take the far corner seriously, and they take it very seriously together.
**Typical contributions:** Vorg theories. Sightings. The insistence that the mystery is
real. Keeps the wonder alive when the adults want to file it under "nothing."
**Running jokes:**
- Has a new vorg theory roughly weekly. Each contradicts the last. All are filed.
- Believes Sakura communicates information that Sakura is, in fact, not communicating.
- Has never seen a vorg. Is more certain of them than of things actually seen.
**Canon reputation:** The keeper of the mystery. The vorg dossier would be half its size
without this correspondent. See `vorg-mythology.md`.

---

## Soren
**Official title:** The Strategist — The Skeptic-in-Residence.
**Narrative role:** The Skeptic and the Strategist. Demands evidence. Doubts the vorg
reports (loudly, and on the record). Plans the patrols that Sakura then ignores entirely.
Keeps the mythology *honest* — every great legend needs one voice asking "but did it
actually happen, though," and that voice belongs to Soren.
**Relationship with Sakura:** Affectionately exasperated. Soren designs efficient patrol
routes; Sakura runs the route she prefers, which is worse, and succeeds by her own metrics
anyway. Soren cannot prove she is wrong. This is maddening and beloved.
**Typical contributions:** Strategy, doubt, the rival theory, the demand for proof. The
counterweight that keeps the canon from floating off into pure fantasy.
**Running jokes:**
- Has "debunked" the vorg several times. The vorg remains. Soren remains unconvinced and
  the vorg remains unconfirmed; both of them are technically winning.
- Builds the optimal plan. Sakura voids the optimal plan. The optimal plan is filed away,
  pristine and unused, a monument to what could have been.
**Canon reputation:** The loyal opposition. The mythology needs the skeptic to have any
weight at all. Soren provides the gravity.

---

## Nolyn
**Official title:** The Wildcard — Liaison to Chaos Mode.
**Narrative role:** The instigator of events. Where Nolyn goes, *something happens* —
several squirrels become involved, a perfectly calm patrol becomes a saga, a quiet
afternoon becomes an incident worth logging. The patron of Chaos Mode and the reason the
event engine exists at all.
**Relationship with Sakura:** Co-conspirator. Sakura's energy and Nolyn's energy combine
into a feedback loop that the rest of the family watches with a mix of joy and bracing.
The two of them have started more backyard legends, accidentally, than anyone.
**Typical contributions:** Chaos. Momentum. The spark. The unscripted moment that becomes
canon precisely because no one planned it.
**Running jokes:**
- "What did you two do?" is a complete sentence in this household and it is always aimed
  here, fondly, after the fact.
- Can turn any one-creature situation into a three-creature situation instantly.
- Has never started chaos on purpose. Chaos simply recognizes a kindred spirit.
**Canon reputation:** Beloved agent of disorder. Chaos Mode is, in the deepest sense,
dedicated to Nolyn.

## Cast Law

- All family members are real people, portrayed only with affection.
- Their in-game personalities should grow toward how they actually are around Sakura —
  these profiles are a starting costume, not a cage.
- Family members provide tips, warnings, briefings, reactions, and the final word on canon.
- No family member is ever the butt of the joke. Sakura's *sincerity* is the joke; the
  family is the loving audience that makes the sincerity matter.
- The tone is, always: affectionate, slightly ridiculous, entirely true.
